Running Up That Hill

Running Up That Hill



After a deafening silence of many years, aside from 'The Single File' box-set and accompanying video, Kate returned to the popscene, with what was to be her most important release since her 'Wuthering Heights'-debut… .


OoOoO


It had been almost three years since her last single, and even longer since her last hit, so a big chart-success was needed. To bring Kate back to the top, and to silence all the critics who had been labelling her a has-been ever since the failure of 'There Goes A Tenner', and of course the album 'The Dreaming', which,at least from a commercial point of view, hadn't exactly lived up to its high expectations.


OoOoO


The single was released in August 1985, and most fans and critics alike seemed very impressed.. .


OoOoO


The reviewers gave it a collective 'Thumbs Up', and here's what they had to say:

"I don't expect there to be any bigger hits this year" - Kerrang

"A monster hit beyond doubt" - Number 1

"It's absolutely brilliant" - Just 17

"I found myself seduced by the sheer strangeness of Ms Bush's dramatic return" - N.M.E

"How to return in style" - Smash Hits


OoOoO


'Running Up That Hill' entered the charts at nine, to reach its highest position of number 3 a few weeks later. The appearances on 'Top Of The Pops', 'Wogan' and different satellite TV-shows certainly helped the promotion, as did the enormous media-attention Kate's return had provoked. "Bush blossoms in love" The Sun shouted from its front-page, and who could blame them? Kate was back, and the world ought to know about it.


OoOoO


Thanks to the generous airplay on American alternative stations, the single even managed to break into the Top 40 in The States, thus finally introducing the American public to Kate Bush and laying the foundations of a whole new fanbase.


OoOoO


Basically the song is about two people who are very much in love. So much that they find it impossible to cope and are both scared of losing eachother. If only they could swap places to know how the other was feeling, perhaps that could take away many fears and insecurities. To a point, the song can also be seen as a way of trying to overcome the fundemental differences between a man and a women, and the communication-barriers between the two.


OoOoO


Musically, this is a very deep song, full of atmosphere and emotion. It has a hook-line that slowly creeps into your unconscious mind until you can't stop singing it. The production is thick and heavy, with layer upon layer of music, and Kate's voice bursting out from underneath it. The synthesizers are very 80's, and probably the only concession Kate has ever made to modern music, but of course not without adapting it to suit her own unique style. A 12" version of "Running Up That Hill" was released as well. The extended remix only adds to the depth and intensity of the original


OoOoO

'Running Up That Hill' had Kate back at the top where she belonged, and in eager anticipation, fans and critics waited for the release of "Hounds Of Love".. An album that was to be another big milestone in Kate's career


OoOoO
