Wow



Kate's 4 th single release - and the second one from 'Lionheart' - saw the light on March 9, 1979, but the story of 'Wow' goes back a lot further than that.. .


OoOoO


Between the 18 th and the 21 st of February, Kate had been in Luzern, Switzerland, where she took part in the recording of the Abba-Tv-Special (best known for the barefoot-in-the-snow version of 'Wow'), and also recorded the routine to her forthcoming video… .


OoOoO


Needless to say the press were there, and in typically British tradition they went wild, over what they considered to be a sexy shocker. 'It's Kate Bush scaling erotic heights with her new song' The Daily Express-headline screamed; 'She's the hottest thing on stage' the Daily Mail declared; in The Sun, the header 'Wow Is The Word' caught everyone's attention..


OoOoO


Somewhere between the 20 th and the 26 th , the final video was made back in Britain. Directed by Keef and recorded at 'Wiltons Music Hall' in East London.


OoOoO


On March 3rd, Kate's tour was announced to the music press., adding even more to the media-circus..

All the publicity could only benefit the new single of course, as Kate seemed to be everywhere.. Magazine-covers, radio, local and national press… . And television.. .On March 5th, Kate's video got its TV-debut on 'The Kenny Everett Show'.. Several television-appearances followed, including a 'Musical Chairs'-special about the making of 'Wow'. Both Kate and Keef were interviewed, and this particular programme is also the only time Kate has ever been seen smoking in public. Something she later regretted, as she tries to avoid being photographed cigarette in hand.


OoOoO


Then, March 9th finally saw the single's official release. Most of the reviewers seemed to like it, a few confused exceptions aside:

"I remain unconvinced by the winsome waif, but her records are so well presented, I usually find myself reluctantly absorbed into their fresh growing luxuriance'"- N M E

"The most focused single since Wuthering Heights" - Melody Maker

"An eerie gentle number" - Record Mirror

"Apart from the body, what does anyone see in this pantomime?" - Sounds

"The girl is incredible" - Super Pop


OoOoO


'Wow' entered the chart at 61, and after many more TV- and radio-appearances, including the screening of the above mentioned Abba-special, it peaked at number 14. Despite the fact that the BBC had deemed it unsuitable for its younger viewers and refused to show it on 'Top Of The Pops' or any other programme, apart from the occasional fragment… .


OoOoO


The original video can still be found on 'The Single File', but for 'The Whole Story', Kate decided to make a new one.. "I don't really like the promo we did for 'Wow'. I think it's silly. And also the tour is a big part of the story, was a big part of my career. I felt I should include something about that.".. So, that's why on 'The Whole Story', the original 'Wow' has been replaced by excerpts from the tour.


OoOoO


A lot has been said about the song. At the time, many people claimed it to be a criticism of homosexuals, but this of course is way off track… Kate herself explains the story as an observation of the shady side of show-business. Starlets sleeping their way to the top, but never getting there; Producers saying how fantastic someone is, but never really meaning it; Actors waiting for that one big break that never comes… Or as Kate put it herself "It can be a very hard and treacherous world out there"… .
