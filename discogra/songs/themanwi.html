The Man With The Child In His Eyes



'Wuthering Heights' had reached number one in Britain and achieved a huge hit-status all over the world (except for the Americas where it took until 1985 to get Kate really noticed) and so EMI were left with the monstrous task of choosing a follow-up.


OoOoO


Their first choice, it appears, was the catchy 'Them Heavy People'. But Kate herself decided she preferred 'The Man With The Child In His Eyes' to be the next single. She found it important to show the listeners the versatility of her work. Or as she put it herself: "I'd like people to listen to it as a songwriting-song as opposed to something weird, which was the reaction to 'Wuthering Heights'. That's why this matters so much to me and why I want the song to do well".


OoOoO


Some people say that this is the nearest Kate ever came to writing a standard pop-type love song. This track is indeed totally opposite to 'Wuthering Heights'. Some people found it very hard to believe that this was the very same girl who only a few weeks earlier had been singing about Cathy's lost love in that very shrill voice… . Which is of course exactly the way Kate wanted it: "If the next song had been similar, I would have been labelled and that something I really don't want. As soon as you get a label, you can't do anything. I prefer to take a risk, and keep my artistic freedom."


OoOoO


It is rumoured that "The Man With The Child In His Eyes" was written when Kate was just twelve, and recorded when she was 16. Even now, the lyrics are still scribbled somewhere on a wall at 'East Wickham Farm" or so we're told.. "The Man…" was in fact one of the many tracks on the demo-tape that got Kate her recording-contract. The track is the same as on the 'The Kick Inside'-album, with the 'he's here, he's here'-vocals added later… .


OoOoO


The song itself is a slow and sensual ballad. A swooning song, with a hint of nostalgia thrown in for good measure. And also a simple song, based just around the piano and Kate's warm voice.


OoOoO


The story behind it deals with the love of a young girl for an older man. He could be someone she's infatuated with - a teacher or a popstar perhaps. He could be a lover. Or then again, he could be just a figment of her imagination. That's for the listener to decide.


OoOoO


The video was very simple and, like the other early ones, directed by Keif. It seems as if Kate is floating on the screen in a mass of white clouds, but actually she's sitting on a perspex box to give that impression. The movements were all her own idea, with added creative input from her brother John. Basically, Kate had the idea of someone sitting down and telling someone something secret. And of course the video also saw the debut of the now famous lurex cat-suit that at the time seemed to receive even more press-attention than the song itself… .


OoOoO


Fortunately, the song didn't go by unnoticed, and all the critics seemed to like it. Well, almost... :

"A clever follow-up" - Melody Maker

"Fascinating and with a distinctive vocal-style" - Sounds

"Before I float away, all I can say is buy it"- Record Mirror

"Just plain dull" - N M E


OoOoO


'The Man With The Child In His Eyes' got to number 6 in the British charts, and has since then been the subject of many cover versions.. Dave Smith And Judy Denny, Dusty Springfield and Natalie Cole to name just a few.


OoOoO


Let's leave Kate the last word about the song that not only consolidated the success of her hit-debut but also proved her versatility as a singer/songwriter:

"He's wise, yet retains a certain innocent quality. Like all the men who are still little boys inside. He's a very real character to her, but no one but she herself knows if he really exists."
