Sat In Your Lap

When 'Sat In Your Lap' was released, it was a new and exciting time for Kate's fans, as her music was going in a completely new direction, and for those fans who were willing to go along with Kate on this journey to new sounds, the rewards were plenty.


OoOoO


Kate said of the song:"I was really frightened about this track for a long time. I mixed the song and played it to people and there was complete silence afterwards. Or someone would say that they liked it to me, and then go away and say what they really thought".


OoOoO


From the opening beats, the song is pushed along at a frightening speed, and you are barely able to take in the lyrics. What you do take in, are the pounding drums, the striking piano, and Kate's sweeping vocal performance…. This was definitely a side of her that we had never seen before, and as far as style goes, people were already starting to wonder whether it had been her ambition all along, to become a female version of Adam Ant.


OoOoO


Another thing, that was rather different about 'Sat InYour Lap' was that it was quite danceable, thanks to the urgent beat of the drums. And indeed, this had been Kate's intention as she later said in an interview: 'I'm starting to connect my dancing to my music more. It's surprising, but in the past I don't think I've been able to grasp the concept of rhythm properly..'


OoOoO


The song is all about searching for knowledge, wanting more, seing things work for others and wanting them to work for you to.. Or as Kate herself put it:'It's all about the quest for knowledge. Realising you actually don't know anything at all. It's important to be reminded, sometimes, that you're insignificant and stupid'.


OoOoO


The video was filmed in just two days, partly in a video-studio, and partly at 'Abby Road'. The clip sees Kate looking very pretty, dressed in a white tutu complete with pink leg-warmers wrapped around both her arms and legs. And then there's of course also the infamous dunce-cap… . It would be an understatemnt to describe the video as hyperactive, seeing there are a thousand things going on all over the place and most of them make very little or no sense to me.. However, having said that, it still remains one of my favourites.


OoOoO


The critics'views on Kate's latest single were, as usual, rather mixed:

'On this single, Kate menaces and pouts like a petulant angel. La Bush lucikily still has a few tricks up her sleeve' - Record Mirror

'Arthritic, artistically constipated, out of touch travesties that should have never seen the light of day' - Melody Maker

'Only Toyah comes to mind as being able to create a more discordant slice of shrieking sub-opera' - Sounds

'Kate has penned some beautiful songs, but when she turns all aggressive and strident, the consequences are frankly excruciating' - N.M.E

'A piano begins in the neighbourhood of take five and La Belle Dame Sians turkeys decorously decides to follow the tune too. This time the dog doesn't have to leave the room. Is this portent?' - Hot Press


OoOoO


The Friday before the release, the single was previewed on BBC Radio 1's "Round Table". Everyone on the show seemed to be in favour of it, and Rick Wakeman even went so far as to compare it to Bowie's classic 'Ashes To Ashes'.

The video was shown on 'Top Of The Pops', well half of it anyway. Kate also appeared on "Razzmatazz" and "Looking Good, Feeling Fit" to furher aid the promotion.


OoOoO


'Sat In Your Lap' got to number eleven in Britain, and all in all it was a very important release for Kate. The ending of one phase and the beginning of another. One question, however, as yet remained unanswered… Would everyone be ready for "The Dreaming"?
