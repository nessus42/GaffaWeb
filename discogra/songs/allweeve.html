All We Ever Look For



'All We Ever Look For', one of the most sensitive tracks on the 'Never Forever'-album, sees Kate questioning the roots of our many philosophies. Our parents simply looked to us, their children, to give their lives meaning, and now we are breaking down over anxieties they never even have the leisure to think about. Maybe it's all just a matter of self-indulgence, wallowing in self-pity… 'Leave the breast and then the nest, and then regret you ever left'…Aren't we all just out in the cold, after all, and looking for a better place, trying to get back to the womb?

Philosophical questions are odd. Even philosophers admit that, and Wittgenstein claimed that there aren't even any real questions… But they do cross everyone's mind at one time or another. It may be a simple reaction to a surprise situation. It could be a genuine dilemma. Maybe it's even a serious morbid psychological doubt.. 'Do I really exist?'


OoOoO


In 'All We Ever Look For' Kate asks herself , she wonders: what are we all, ultimately, hoping to find? Are they the real answers, or just a bit of comfort.. 'A God, a drug, a great big hug'… . A little piece of someone else…


OoOoO


'All We Ever Look For' has a very distinctive sound - almost like a country dance or an Elizabethan gavotte. The tune features some kind of whistling. Almost certainly a human whistle played back on the 'Fairlight' with unnatural precision.


OoOoO


In the middle of the song, we hear the sound of someone running down corridors from room to room, searching for an answer… And I can't help but wonder - could those hollow sounds of footsteps have been recorded in the stone-floored passages at 'East Wickham Farm'?
