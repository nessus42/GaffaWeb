The Pic Inside



So, what do you do? There, on the stall at the record-fair sits "The Kick Inside" picture disc. Can you really justify spending £50-60 on it? What if it's the second pressing and not the original issue? Hey, wait a minute! Nobody told me there were two pressings! How do I tell the difference? Does it really matter? Well, it doesn't really matter I suppose, unless they're asking the original's price for the second pressing. Price-wise the first pressing is generally worth £10-20 more than the re-issue. And the same certainly goes for some of Kate's early British singles. So, here's a small guide...


Title Original Re-Issue
The Kick Inside - UK Pic.disc A Side: The copyright note starts at Kate's foot and ends at her fingertips A Side: The writing has wider spacing and starts before her foot and goes past her fingertips.
Wuthering Heights Back cover: EMI logo is circular. G+L printed on bottom right. EMI logo is square. No G+L
The Man With The Child In His Eyes G+L logo is clearly visible on bottom right of back cover G+L in bottom right of back cover is very faint.
Hammer Horror The folds at the top and bottom have rounded edges The folds are cut at an angle and have straight edges
On Stage EP (Gatefold sleeve) The overlap of the sleeve is virtually invisible. Easily found upward overlap on bottom edge cut at angle.
Wow Folds on both edges have rounded corners. EMI logo is circular. Angled edges on folds and square EMI logo.
Breathing Folds on back cover have rounded edges on bottom. Folds on back cover have straight edges on bottom.
Babooshka Folds on back cover have angled straight edges at bottom Folds on back cover are straight all the way down
December will be magic again Credits for cut, cover, songs and arrangements are directly below the B-side-info Credits appear on bottom of cover, approx. 1 inch lower than they should be.

Apart from these differences, all The Single File singles are in paper-type sleeves rather than the card of the originals, so that should give you a clue when buying.
