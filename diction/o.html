O'Flynn, Liam

 A founding member of the group Planxty, O'Flynn plays Uillean pipes on the songs Night Of The Swallow, Jig Of Life, and Hello Earth, and pennywhistle on the song Night Of The Swallow.

Oh England My Lionheart

A song on the album Lionheart. The term "Lionheart" seems to have a special attraction for Kate; apart from its use in this song and as an album title, she composed one other unreleased song with that word (Where Are The Lionhearts?) and has referred to her fans as "Lionhearts." The title of this song was parodied in England My Leotard.

"Peter Pan steals the children in Kensington Park" refers to a statue of the character which can be seen in a playground in Kensington Gardens, the western part of Hyde Park in London. The playground was actually donated to the city by J.M. Barrie, the author of Peter Pan.

This song is also listed in the Discography section.

Ono, Yoko

At an auction at Sotheby's on 22 December 1981, Kate purchased a perspex statue of John Lennon and Yoko Ono patterned after the cover of their Two Virgins album, along with a copy of the shooting script for the Beatles film Magical Mystery Tour.

Orgonon

A 200 acre estate in Maine, founded in 1942 and home to the Wilhelm Reich Foundation, as mentioned in the song Cloudbusting. The name has sometimes been misspelled "Organon" on lyric sheets.

[Back to Index Page]

Othello

A tragedy written by William Shakespeare (1564 - 1616). The song Blow Away quotes from Act V, Scene II:
"Put out the light, and then put out the light.
If I quench thee, thou flaming minister,
I can again thy former light restore"
The lyric "vibes in the sky invite you to dine" is, however, pure Kate.
