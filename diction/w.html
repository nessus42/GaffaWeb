Wall, The

This concept album by Pink Floyd provided helicopter sounds for the song Waking The Witch.

Kate on The Wall, ca. 1982:

"I've been very much influenced by The Wall because I like the way that the Floyd get right into that emotional area and work with sounds as pictures. I think the problem with the film, though, is that although as a piece of art it is devastating, it isn't real enough. The whole film is negatively based. Not once during Pink's life is there a moment of happiness, which I know in every human's life there is. Even if you have the shittiest life of all, there is always one little moment where you smile for a second or you fall in love with someone and feel happy -- maybe only for ten minutes. In The Wall there is no compassion and no objectivity at all, and I actually think that certain areas of that are destructive."

Warchild

A charity project organized by Brian Eno for which Kate has donated a variety of fund-raising artwork. In addition to designing a series of Christmas cards, Kate created the sculpture Strange Fruit in tribute to Billie Holiday as part of an exhibit of musicians' art based on their own musical idols. Other participating celebrities included Eno, Luciano Pavarotti, Gary Barlow, Lou Reed and Yoko Ono. The works were exhibited to the general public at the Economist Plaza in St. James Street, London, and went to auction on 4 February 1997.

Whips

A wooden percussion instrument used in an orchestra to simulate the sound of a whip crack. Two slabs of wood, connected by a hinge, are brought together quickly to produce a loud slap. What did you expect?

Paddy Bush is credited with playing this instrument on the song The Sensual World, but this is a mistake; he is in fact swinging a pair of large fishing rods back and forth, creating the whooshing sound heard on the track.

A song on the album The Red Shoes. Wilde, Oscar

(b. Oscar Fingal O'Flahertie Wills Wilde, 1854 - 1900) An Irish poet, dramatist, essayist and author. In addition to the novel The Picture Of Dorian Gray, Wilde wrote several books of children's stories, as well as plays such as Lady Windermere's Fan and The Importance Of Being Earnest. A hugely popular public figure at one time, and one of the primary influences on the art world before the turn of the century, he fell into disgrace in 1895 when he was sentenced to two years of imprisonment after his unsuccessful lawsuit against the Marquis of Queensbury. As a result of this experience he subsequently wrote The Ballad Of Reading Gaol. Wilde died in exile in Paris.

Kate has cited Wilde as an influence and favorite author on many occasions, singling out his story The Happy Prince as a special favorite. Wilde is named in the song December Will Be Magic Again:

"Light the candle lights
to conjure Mister Wilde
into the Silent Night
Ooh, it's quiet inside
here in Oscar's mind."

Winter Snowtime Special, The

Following her appearance on the Abba Easter Special, Kate was a guest on this second holiday-themed special which aired on 21 December 1979. Kate had originally recorded a version of Wuthering Heights standing barefoot on the Swiss Alps, but this footage was not used, and a hastily prepared version of December Will Be Magic Again was aired instead. The following week, she performed the song again on her own television special Kate; a studio version of the song was not released until nearly a year later.
