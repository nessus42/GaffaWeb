> And, for your files, it is not 'the Eurythmics' any more than it
> is 'the Talking Heads', who actually titled an album to clear up
> any misunderstanding. Of course, people still get it wrong.

Then explain why Chris Frantz in the movie "Stop Making Sense" says,
"It's time for us to turn back into THE Talking Heads."  (At the end
of Tom-Tom Club's "Genius Of Love".  Oops, I'm sorry.  That's "THE
Tom-Tom Club.")

In the words of Sir Winston Churchill, "That is the sort of pedantry
up with which I shall not put."  (Said upon the event of a woman
having corrected him when he ended a sentence with a prepostion.)

> Sorry, but it is just as easy to get these things right, you know.....

.....But only if you're a big enough fan of the group to care.

If you were a TRUE Heads fan in 1980, you had to try to pronounce the
inverted "A's".  Or, upon the release of "SP EAK IN GI N TO NGU ES",
you had to pronounce their name "TA LKI N GHE ADS".  I mean, they went
to the trouble to use that spacing on the record label *and* on the
spine of the album, so they must have wanted their name pronounced
that way.  Right?
