> What does The Neon Judgement sound like?

Hmmn, that's hard; one thing I like about `Horny As Hell' is that it
is so varied, each song is *very* different from the one before and
after; a rough stab is Robert Smith fronting Sisters of Mercy doing a
Prince cover (?!?!?!)  (`I Stood In Between'), Suicide (`S.T.L.G.'),
Fields of The Nephelim-ish Ennio Morricone Telecaster-twang (`The
Sea', `Miss Brown'), Sakamoto-esque Far Eastern rhythms with New
Orderish bass and Peter Gabriel `Shock The Monkey'-type wailing over
the top (`Horny As Hell'), good heavy drum machine dance floor
freakout (`The Bad Luck'), and caliope-esque post-funk with Frankie
Goes To Hollywood lyrical concerns (`Trashy Lady').  How's that?
Hell, it's only $6 or $7 on Play It Again Sam USA, the pressing's
good, I like it a lot, take a chance.

While you're at it, get the Borghesia `No Hope No Fear' ... sounds
like what you'd get if Skinny Puppy had come from Yugoslavia instead
of Vancouver, and had been to a lot of Laibach gigs ... real good
stuff.
