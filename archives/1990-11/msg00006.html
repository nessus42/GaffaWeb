I was playing some of my new musical aquisitions for a photographer friend of
mine last night and she started talking about John Carder's (assumption) shot 
for _Aspects_. Anyways, I ended up dragging out almost every KaTe photo I have 
and Sue had some interesting comments about the Option cover. The cover shot 
was done with a wide-angle lens from very close up. This causes several bad 
things. One is a fishbowl effect which mildly distorts KaTe's face. In 
addition, the focus of wide-angle lens is kind of the surface of a sphere. 
If you look at the cover you can easily see, once its pointed out :), that only
KaTe's lips (a good feature), and cheeks (usually not *anyone's* good feature)
are really in focus. Her nose is blurred out (and distorted by the lens) and 
her *eyes* (her *best* feature IMHO, and the seat of expression) are out of 
focus, blurred, vague, and yes even *blank*. 

Looking back at John Carder's covers, you'll notice that he uses lighting to 
produce a highlight in Kate's eyes (amoung other things) in almost every case.
This emphasizes her eyes and puts a 'spark' into them. The shots by Kevin 
Cummins in Option are sadly lacking this extra touch. Now I'm not saying that 
a smile wouldn't have made a difference, I'm just saying that the photographer 
could have made this shot much better with a better choice of lighting and 
equipment.
