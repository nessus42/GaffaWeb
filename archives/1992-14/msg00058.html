Good things about the box set:

- instantly gives you the complete EMI recordings of kate, minus three
instrumental versions.
- sexy black box fits in well with trendy black furniture and stereo
equipment
- nice picture book with glossy drool-resistant pages
- UK editions of CDs have better sound than US editions
- Never For Ever CD has correct track indexing unlike US edition

Bad things about the box set:

- too expensive
- pointless sticker sheet.  you can't use them because it ruins the
integrity of your box set.  so what do you do with them, then?  if i ever
went on tour or played live again i might slap one on my keyboard or guitar
and ruin the integrity of my set just so i could look cool.
- you gotta paw through the stickers and book to get at the CDs and they
aren't the easiest to get out, particularly if you've been letting your
fingernails grow long.
- too expensive
- nothing new.  most collectors have all the tracks already.
- weird size and shape guarantees it won't fit in your CD rack.

Mitigating factors:

- if you can sell your old kt merchandise, you can defray the cost
somewhat.  i got the box set from a canadian contact for something like
US$130.  sold all my old KT discs for a total of $40, bringing the price on
the box down to $90, which isn't that bad for 8 import CDs.
- those b-sides sound great on CD.  except passing through air which sounds
really muddy and compressed.  but i love it anyway.  what a dopey song.
wheee!

any more questions?
