notes--

Virginia Astley, Kendra Smith, Judie Tzuke and Hayzee Fantayzee and
The Sundays were requests (my listeners request NEAT things!).

Bel Canto's new album is very good, but I still like the first one
(White Out Conditions) best. The second one will end up sounding
dated in a few years. I'm convinced that won't happen to the first.

Caterwaul is a band I've fallen in love with, but I don't recommend them.
Why? Because they're a "either love 'em or hate 'em" band and I don't
want to be responsible for someone going out and buying their albums and
ending up using them as frisbees. It's hard to guess who will like them.
"Ethereals" and people who love truly unusual female voices will probably
love Betsy Martin's vocals but might be put off by the heavy guitar-based
rock. "Rockers" who'll listen to nearly anything with guitars & drums might
despise Betsy's voice and vocal style. It's impossible for me to say "you
will like this" because not everyone is as weird as I am :-)

Marta Sebestyen & Muzsikas' albums I will recommend to anyone who
likes ethnic/folk/foreign language music. It's beautiful, haunting,
fun, would appeal to anyone who likes the Bulgarian Chorus and Najma.
