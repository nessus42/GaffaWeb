>It's too bad that Geffin Records decided that thier profits were more
>important than Peter Gabriel's wish to call #4 "Peter Gabriel" as well.

Wasn't it released as "Peter Gabriel" in Europe? I thought that "Security"
was just slapped on for the North American release.

>Then his idea of having his albums be titled like a serial magazine,
>where the issue number changes instead of the title, would still be going
>on.

And just why should records have titles, anyway? Unless the album represents
a coherent piece of music (eg, The Wall) or concept (eg, SPLHCB) or both
(eg, TNW), it is usually just a collection of songs which doesn't really 
merit a title. The idea of picking the most mellifluous title or the song 
with the biggest hit-potential and naming the album after it always struck 
me as bogus. In general, titles for albums are just convenient labels.
Peter Gabriel had the right idea, say I.

Julian
