Now, to all:  Yes, I should have said Sandman #53 (hey, just be glad I
spelled "Sandman" correctly!).  Mea culpa.

Also, I know that Kate based "The Handsome Cabin Boy" on an Olde English
Folk Song, but let's consider some facts:

1)  Neil Gaiman is known to like Kate (I'll have to go look up the exact
issue, I think it's 4 or 5 - it's in the "Preludes and Nocturns" anthology);
after all, he quotes her ("See the sun rise in the hand of the man.") as
one of the character's dying words.  I'll have to look that up, too (Geez,
who do you think I am, IED, the Cecil Adams of Katefans?)

2)  One can not like Kate without being, as IED so wonderfully put it,
a "fucking Kate fanatic".

3) One can therefore assume that Neil is indeed a KateFan (tm).  So he's
undoubtedly aware of the Kate Song "The Handsome Cabin Boy".

4)  Neil like Tori Amos.  While I know a few Katefans (tm) who don't like
Tori, I've yet to meet anyone who likes Tori but not Kate.  This strengthens
point 3.

5)  Neil is one of those smug literary Brits, so he's undoubtedly aware of
the Olde English folksong "The Handsome Cabin Boy" (Hell, he probably knows
the deleted verses, too!)  My prediction:  In an upcoming issue, one of those
characters who've lived for hundereds of years will be... Kate Bush!  (Who
will be, of course, lovers with Hob, or something like that...)

- Stev0 the Logical
