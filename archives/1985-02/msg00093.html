I haven't read it but I did just get finished rereading "Remember
Bix" which is a bio on Bix Beiderbecke (sp?).  It's amazing 
how the more things change the more they stay the same.  Bix's
life looks more like a rock musicians (ala Jimi, Keith Moon) than
what you would think a standard jazz tooter/viper would be.

All in all, a good book (don't remember the authors name) cause it
also describes (for all you drummers) how the hi-hat was invented,
the tyranny of Paul Whiteman, gay/drag-queen lifestyles in the
1920's-30's and a personal account of a young man discovering
marihuana, free sex and the automobile.  Good reading really.  Wouldn't
be suprised if you can find it at the local library.
