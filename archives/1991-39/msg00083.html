No, I've never heard of them- at least, not that I know of. :)  As far as what
can be termed "Medieval", that depends whose book you're reading.  My personal
dates are from ca.800-1517, that is from Charlemagne to the Reformation, but
there are those who say flat-out 400-1400, 800-1789, and lots of other spots in
between.

As far as "Medieval music" goes, that encompasses the German Minnesang, the
songs of the Troubadours, the Crusades, and of course Gregorian Chant and
Plainsong.  Once you get into (more) complicated string arrangements and funky
vocal lines, you're into the Renaissance, Elizabethan, and Baroque periods...

Judging just from the dates, I'd say Miranda Sex Garden does Renaissance and
very early Baroque, even Elizabethan (depending on what language it's in :).
I'll still check it out, though.  Thanks!!! :)
