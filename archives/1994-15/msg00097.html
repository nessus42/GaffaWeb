    This may be due to the fact that, even though it is international,
love-hounds is primarily American. As someone already pointed out, the
Top Ten were all singles.

    The vast majority of Konvention attendees were from the UK.
    Singles are more important in the UK.
    Kate has hardly had any singles in the US.
    
    Kate is marketed and viewed quite differently in the US than the UK.
She is truely an "album" artist in the US, and her albums didn't really
start to sell here until _The Dreaming_. A whole bunch of factors.

    Also, Konvention attendees tended to be a self-selected group of
Kate's most ardent fans, and the UK fans probably first heard of Kate
with _Wuthering Heights_ rather than (as in the US) _Running Up That
Hill_. A list produced from US fans for a US Con would be quite
different.
