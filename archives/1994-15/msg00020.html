>>...I would like to know how many are subscribing....

> I too am curious about this.  I was on Happy Rhodes mail list for 
> a brief time and I remember seeing a list posted periodically giving
> the names of all the subscribers. (May have included emails and

Hi, I just wanted to clear this up. A full list of subscribers is *never*
posted to Ecto, and is not public knowledge. As far as I know, only 2
people have access to the entire subscribers list, and neither would
ever post it or make it public in any way.

Lurkers are welcome and don't have to worry about their name being made
public if they don't want it to be made public.

I'm certain that what you saw was the Birthday list, which Klaus Kluge keeps
and posts. The people who are on the list have *asked* to be added to the 
list, knowing that it will be posted.

Like Bill Wisner, I don't think it's a good idea to make subscriber's
lists public, and I just didn't want anyone to think that Ecto is an
exception to that basic rule of privacy.
