"om mani padme hum" isn't Latin.  It's Sanskrit.  Probably the most well-
known Hindu mantra, or chant.  "om" and "hum" are "power words", which have
no ordinarily recognized meaning, although "om" or more correctly "aum"
supposedly represents the continuing process of creation, existence and
destruction.  "mani padme" means literally "the jewel is in the lotus".
Symbolically, it represents union of the human with the divine.  In some
sects (no pun intended), it also represents sexual union, the jewel standing
for the lingam, or phallus, and the lotus for the yoni, or vagina.  BTW,
"aum" is also used frequently as a mantra in its own right.
