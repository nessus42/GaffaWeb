Also, since "Under the Ivy" has been getting a lot of time - it seems
important to determine who is addressed when Kate sings "It's not easy for
me to give away your secret".  I have always assumed she was talking to
"the little girl" inside her who prompts her retreat to her secret garden
spot.  This would support the notion that the tryst was an adult's
romantic encounter and that by bringing someone else there she is
"betraying" a part of her childhood, a secret place where only she was
allowed to be.
