	The vocals Chris is talking about start at about 1:31 into Under Ice
in the left channel.  I agree that the word "sonar" is easily distinguishable.
After much playing with it, I've determined that it's easiest to hear the words
by dropping the low bands on the eq. (and the right channel, of course), but I 
still can't figure out what else he is saying.
	On a semi-related topic, has anybody gone through the trouble to
digitize Waking the Witch (onto computer disk) and splice the chopped vocals 
together to figure out what is being said (or am I the only one who can't 
figure them out)?
	On a non-related topic, a friend of mine has a copy of _Steal This Disk
2_.  There's a song on it by Birdsongs of the Mesozoic (Theme From Rocky &
Bullwinkle from the Sonic Geology album).  I wan't too terribly impressed with
the song, but it has one odd characteristic which we hadn't noticed for a LONG
time.  The song preceding this one on the disk wasn't too great, so we always
skipped to the beginning of the BotM song.  If you don't listen to it straight
through, then you don't notice that the BotM song starts at -1:32 NOT at 0:00.
Incedently, the song is only 1:29 long, which makes this "intro" longer than
the actual song (it's 1:32 of introduction of the upcoming song spoken forward,
backward, and a number of other bizarre manners, splashed together in a sort of
a musical collage...quite interesting).  My question is:  is this done on the
Sonic Geology disk as well?  Do they do this sort of thing often?  Has anyone
else done anything bizarre like this?
	Oh, and one last thing, way back (7-10 months ago) someone posted that
he had Kate's lyrics in *roff format.  I have since lost the message, but am
still very interested in getting copies of these.  Can anyone out there help me
out?  Thanks.
