One of the things I came away with after seeing /+) in Shepherdstown
(twice!) was that the portion of the film where she is spinning through the
snowstorm touching all her friends who've passed on was one of the best
Kate bits on film EVER!  In terms of visual images supporting the emotional
statement that the singer is making, without resorting to just a literal
interpretation of the words, this sequence packed more wallop than anything
else from before.  The simplicity of the execution is one important factor.
Another is the restraint with which the sequence is set up...the graceful
monotony of Kate just spinning through the sky which is kind of a metaphor
for her whole life (Passing thru Air, Dervishes, Big Sky etc.)  This moment
of the song had real feelings behind it, and Kate does justice to it
visually as well as aurally, IMHO.
