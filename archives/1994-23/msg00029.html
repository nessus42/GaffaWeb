Here's some info from the April/May issue of CD-ROM Today magazine
published here in the UK.  It's from an article about Video CD
which is described as a 5" disk capable of holding up to 65 minutes
of video using MPEG-1 compression.  If this sound's like what
you've got the bad news is.....

   "There is also a limited number of machines that you can play
Video CD disks on.  A Philips CD-i player fitted with their Digital
Video Cartridge is one option; the Commodore Amiga CD32 games console
fitted with their Digital Video Cartridge is another; and a PC fitted
with the ReelMagic digital video board is the only other choice....."

They go on to say that the ReelMagic card will soon be available
for Macs as well.
