No No I dont think this was HAWKWIND, as doug has already said they have
never had a female vocalist.  I've been told that a group called SPACE
had a minor hit in the seventies so that could have been the group that
you where listening to.  HAWKWIND have been through several types of
music ( Psychadelia, heavy metal, punk and evan god forbid a little bit
of funk (the aubegine that eat rangoon)) but this discription really
doesnt sound at all like them.
	Doug mentioned that they like to play their synths using the
knobs and not the keyboards, well although partly true it is not the
whole story, in the early days of the HAWKS (1969 -> 71) the electronic
sounds where created using frequency generators ( yes the sort that you
will find in any electronics lab, sine wave output, frequency controll,
amplitude control and that was it).  They then advanced on to a
analogue synth called the VCS3 which still had lots of knobs but could
also use a keyboard!!! (this synth was also used by GONG, STEVE
HILLAGE, HERE and NOW and PINK FLOYD on the dark side of the moon
album).By now HAWKWIND have advanced on to modern digital synths BUT
they still use analogue synths (korg ms20 i think) for the elecronic
noises.
