>small point but I didn't notice it until this morning (when feeling
>yet another aftershock BTW). It's this type of detail that I really

mm, i can't actually remember seeing a discussion on that, but i know
at the very least that this humble footah has ranted and raved about
the UTTER BRILLIANCE of this song :)  musically and lyrically, it is an
interlude, a bit of calm in the stormy waves, but listen to the
textures KaTe (and company, with a special nod towards Jon Kelly)
layer over the simple chords and melody...  i wish i had the CD with
me, so i could accurately point out the many astoundingly lovely
details, but suffice it for me to say now that i think this song and
the entire 9th wave are the most extraordinary examples of the use of
modern studio/electronic music techniques i have yet heard, and that
includes "The Dreaming" and a *lot* of other albums :)

(to head off trivial responses, let me note that i find "The Dreaming"
to be of greater musical interest, and the the two albums are tied in
my heart for personal and emotional significance, but i think KaTe's
understanding of her tools was much greater, and her application of
them more sophisticated, on the 9th Wave)

hmm, perhaps She, angered by the comparisons between Herself and Tori
