just BTW, but a "gaffer" is a person who rigs lights for movies, 
etc. -- they're the people who drop wrenches on the lighting designer
when s/he decides that all those red gels need to be replaced with
blue ones...  real "gaffer's tape" is usually cloth-based, designed 
to withstand the intense heat of professional lamps, tends to
be black, and doesn't leave a goopy residue all over your equipment
and self.  the silvery, plastic, gooey, prone-to-melting-and-wadding-
up stuff you find in the average hardware store gets it's name, 
duck tape, from its use in wild fowl hunting -- in the wilds of
Minnesota you can vast webs of the stuff, with the hapless birds
firmly ensnared...
