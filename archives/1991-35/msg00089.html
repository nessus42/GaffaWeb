> Vickie asks:

>>It's a Fairlight making the sound, right?

And Jeff replies: 

> Um, would you except a "probably"?  I honestly have no idea, though it would
> certainly make sense for it to be a sample.  I can't imagine KaTe
> bringing in a soldier and telling him to, "watch the conductor and slam
> the bolt on your rifle as marked on this sheet."  (though it might have
> been rather funny to watch...;-)  On the other hand, I find it kind of 
> doubtful that that sound would be a preset on a Fairlight, so she must have 
> gotten the sample from a real gun.

And I add:

I don't think that sound would be preprogrammed onto a Fairlight either, but
she must've gotten it from somewhere!  (Profound statement alert)  Probably not
right in the studio, but I can see her going out to a rifle range and taping
the sound of some club member cocking their gun.  It's not that hard to tape
weird "incidental" noises like that.  

Didn't I read somewhere that the percussion in "Get Out Of My House" is 
partially the recorded sounds of each door in Kate's house slamming?  I can 
see it now: "Okay Jay, really wham that one- remember, it slides on the carpet
a bit- >WHUMP<- great!  Now let's try the bathroom!"
