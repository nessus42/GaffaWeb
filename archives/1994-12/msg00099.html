Hi,
 
A while ago I received a videotape from an English guy I know. He'd made
some recordings from "UK Gold", an English Tv station that re-broadcasts old
Englsh programs. Amongst other things, the tape featured "Wuthering heights"
thrice. I am still searching to find the answer to my question: where are
these recordings from?
I read the Garden (GARDEN02; recommended!), but it didn't answer this
question either.
Now I know it's hard, but I'll try to tell you how these recordings looked.
 
One of them features Kate singing the song from behind a piano, on which
some flowers(?) are put.
The other two feature Kate standing up, and singing Wuthering heights live
before an audience, I believe they're sitting behind tables.
In all of these recordings Kate is wearing dark stuff, dresses if I recall
correctly (I am doing this from memory - I saw the lot a few days ago). In
none of these Kate wears the white dress from the clip or the "watching
myself die"-clothing (red slacks and such).
 
Still, in all of these recordings she's presented as British number one,
which suggests it's from some chart show, be it Top of the Pops or something
else. And it must be British as it was broadcast on UK Gold....
 
I'm confused! Does anyone have any ideas?
 
Oh, and what about "A rose growing old"? Why is it called "Feeling like a
waltz" in The Garden? IED? Anyone?
