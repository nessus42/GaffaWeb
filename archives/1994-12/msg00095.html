I think your posting is a a level-headed and reasonable response, but I
would like to point out an important flaw in the very natural response that
people often make in considering gender and race issues.  When looking at a
statement or joke or image, men (in the case of gender) often respond with
the genune defense that:

		"I wouldn't mind if they said that about me."

Insofar as we often appeal to the "Do unto others" rule, this seems a fair
observation. The problem, though, is that such statements never have the
same impact on the "priveliged" group (here, males) as they do on the
offended group.  Usually, groups of people who are in positions of power are
less vulnerable than those who are on the receiving end of jokes and
demeaning name-calling.  For instance, consider the difference between
calling a black person "nigger" and calling a white person "whitey" (or any
other white expletive).
	If you "don't mind" being called something, but someone else does,
you might consider what accounts for the difference. 
	This may be going a bit far off the  topic of this newsgroup,
(though not so far, it seems to me, as some other postings). In itself,
the "fox" issue is pretty small potatoes, but the way we think about it has
broader implications.

Again, Ridley (?), I hope you won't take this as a personal attack.  I think
your heart is in the right place.  I've seen this line of defense pretty
often though, and thought it would be worth responding to.

I'll risk a smiley face --my prose probably sounds pretty self-important.8-)
