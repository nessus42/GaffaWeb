Greetings.  This is my first post, so I hope I don't muck things up too bad.

I have a Kate picture disc (vinyl) that has no liner notes whatsoever, so I
was wondering if one of you knowledgable Kate fans could help me out with
some info.  The picture is a head shot from one of the Hounds of Love
promotional posters.  The concert is a fairly well-known one (as Kate concerts
go), and features the "Another Day" duet with Peter Gabriel, as well as Peter
singing "Here Comes the Flood".  I assume it's a Christmas show, as it also
features "December Will Be Magic" as well as another more traditional 
Christmas song.  I can identify all the other tracks but one.  The song starts
with the line, "Where is she when the little thing cries", and the chorus goes
something like:

	Ohh. Ohh.
	Why did I ever go marry her?  I was a child.
	I'm holding the baby..(something)
	She saw me coming for miles.
	She saw me OPEN WIDE (?)

I hope that's enough to go on.  I was just curious if this is a Kate tune,
or a cover, and what the name is.  Also, I have the Cathy Demos #2-5 on
colored vinyl 7-inch...was wondering if these were ever compiled onto a
single CD? (Not that I'd ever be able to find it if one exists). 

Also, a few years ago, a band called "The Big Dish" released an album called
"Swimmer".  A friend at the time who was working in the record industry
told me that Kate sang backup vocals on a few tracks, but was not credited
on the album for reasons I now forget.  There ARE backup vocals that sound
like they could be her's, but I'm not convinced.  Any truth to this?
