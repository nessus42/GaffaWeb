"The Complete Mike Oldfield" contains various new and unreleased material.
For starters, "Crime Of Passion" and "Jungle Gardenia", originally from
a 1983 12" single, are on this album, as well as the extended version to
"Shadow On The Wall"; remixed excerpts from "Tubular Bells", "Hergest
Ridge", "Ommadawn" and "Incantations" (remixed to bring out musical points
and subtleties missed on the original albums); and four unreleased excerpts
from live performances in 1981, 1982, 1983 & 1984, which I might add are
exceptional.  The rest of the double album is made up of various selections
from his past, including his chart-hitters (Mistake, Moonlight Shadow).
(p.s. Hard-to-find material, such as the singles "Waldberg (The Peak)" and
the theme to the "Blue Peter" children's TV show are also on "TCMO".)

If you're a true Oldfield enthusiast, this album is a must.  The album 
itself contains a brief musical biography of Mike, following his progress
with each album of his career.  (The album cover, also, is striking to
look at.)

Finally, I would be grateful to hear from any and all Oldfield addicts
around here -- voice your comments, questions and scoops!
