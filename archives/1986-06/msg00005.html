Gone/Painted Willie/Scream/Black Flag/St. Augistine's School/ 4/4
Drug of the Evening: some blotter bought off the street.

St. Augustine's school is your run of the mill elementry school,
perfect for this show (ie mentality of the audience is about that
level).  While the old folks (people over 30) played Bingo next door,
we cranked to some of the best Metal in years.  GONE features (ready
boys and girls) Gregg Ginn of Black Flag on Guitar, and Sim Cain and
Andrew (damn, left the tour notes at home) playing drums and bass,
previously with the band Regressive Aid !!! ha ha ha.  They were pretty
awesome, I thought Larry Penis-face would be there, since this trio
played only instrumentals.  There 20+ minutes of jamming included parts
of the song 'Rock and Roll'.  You remember that one, don't you ?? Yea,
that's why I was looking for Larry.

Painted Willie was so-so. I hit the liquor  store for some beer and sat
out with some winos killing time before Scream showed up.  Hot and
heavy as usual.  Read some of the latest Henry Rollins book "Polio
Flesh" between sets.  good reading. All the little boys and girls were
mad because this wasn't punk rock. awwwww. too bad.  Most of the Black
Flag material was from "In My Head" and "Loose Nut".   Quote of the
evening from Henry "Hey man, don't fight. If you fight you'll smash
your face up and then you can't pose anymore".

Robyn Hitchock and the Egyptians/Morning Glories/3/28
Drug of the evening: Mushrooms

Of course I made the mistake of eating these suckers while waiting for
tickets, and almost blew the trip in line.  Of course as soon as I was
moving, I started running into all these people I haven't seen in
awhile, like Labovitz and my presnt editor, who thought I lost my
mind.  Well, I did, kinda.  Morning Glories, featuring Mitch Parker,
were primo and impressed.  Of course Robyn was Robyn, he was good, they
plyed tight, and his stories had my head spinning.  Was it his shirt
that was doing this to me or what ??? Couldn't focus on too much when I
was leaving, my head kept spinning.
