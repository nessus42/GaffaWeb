My sentiments exactly!!!  They are also a fantastic band live.  I saw them at
'The Metro', a Melbourne nightclub, where the place was bursting at the seams
with very passionate fans.  When the band rocked, the whole place was blur of
motion.  When they slowed down to a ballad, the crowd relaxed and immersed
themselves with the songs.  It was quite poinient to see a crowd, a very young
crowd, completely surrender themselves to the band and the music.  They are a
very tight unit and excellent musicians.

Concrete Blond definately perform some of the most powerful and aggressive
songs around.  They put a lot of these so called 'Heavy Metal' bands in the
their place!!

Oh, by the way, Andrew Prieboy (sp?), was a support act.  He did a duet
with Johnette singing 'Tomorrow, Wendy'.  Their vocals went perfectly together
with this song.  That was to me the absolute highlight ot the night.  He also
sang the Led Zeppelin song 'A whole lot of love' to what I thought was a
Honky-tonk beat (think about it!) - just him and his keyboard.  It was
hilarous!!

It was a great and memorable night.
