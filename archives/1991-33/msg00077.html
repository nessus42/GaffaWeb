As a small note, as an act of devotion I was checking out references to the
Ninth Wave in the varsity library, starting with the Tennyson bit and working
my way back. What I finally found out was that Nine in ancient Celtic religion
was the number of the Moon, and since the Moon controls the tides, the Ninth
Wave was always the largest. In one poem the flower-woman Blodeuwedd says 
"Long and white are my fingers as the ninth wave of the sea"... Tennyson 
obviously picked up this motif, and made the baby Arthur ride in on the curl of
the Ninth Wave to the waiting Merlin.
