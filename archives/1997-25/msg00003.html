"Webster" is a generic, non-proprietary tradename like "Acme."

It has no more to do with Noah Webster than it does to do with the sit-com
character Webster. It's meaningless when discussing the value of a
dictionary or the accuracy of the definition of a word in a "Webster's"
dictionary.

I use "The Random House Dictionary of the English Language - 2nd Edition -
Unabridged."

It's about 2500 pages long, but having been published in 1987, it's getting
it a bit long in the tooth...particularily if you're looking for
definitions of computer terms.
