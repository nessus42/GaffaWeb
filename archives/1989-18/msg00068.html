  David Shapiro asks for Vaughan Williams
recommendations. I really like one disc I
have which combines "Ten Blake Songs" and
"On Wenlock Edge" with a piece by Warlock
called "The Curlew".  Another fine selection
is "Five Tudor Portraits," although I don't
have a recording of it.

  My personal experience with Vaughan Williams is that you can't go wrong! 
All of the symphonies are worth listening to, I especially like the fifth
(available on Virgin Classics I believe) and the Symphonia Antartica (sp?). 
The "London" Symphony is very good, I think that's #2.   Anyone who makes a
habit of listening to Vaughan Williams will also come across "The Wasps",
which appears on the radio now and then, and is program music patterned after
the play by Aristophanes. It's fun.

   On the other hand, if it is vocal music you are looking for, there is
plenty to choose from and I suggest you pick something whose source interests
you, "Job" for instance. I bought "Ten Blake Songs" because I like Blake as
well as Vaughan Williams, and I wasn't disappointed.

   Enjoy your discovery!
   Julian
