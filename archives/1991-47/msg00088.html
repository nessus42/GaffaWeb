For thoses who just arrived:

This started when someone mentioned the line "fools rush in where angels
fear to tread". I meantioned that I had read a story whose moral was "fools
rush in where angels fear to tread, but the angels are in heaven, and few of
the fools are dead."  I said it was from a book by Jules Feiffer, but that I
couldn't remember the name. Then we got into a discussion about whether 
Feiffer was spelled with a P or not (I checked, it isn't).

Now for the final chapter. I asked my dad, and he reminded me that it wasn't
from a Jules Feiffer book. It was from a book called "Further Fables for our
Time" by James Thurber (no P).

Just so this post has something about Kate in it. I would like to add my name
to the list of people who like the two new songs. I like CitW better because
it is a sparser arrangment. I wish she would do an album of just vocals and
piano.

Michael
