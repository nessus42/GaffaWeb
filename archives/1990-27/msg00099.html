The First of a Hundred Copies?

From: nrc@cbema.att.com (Neal R Caldwell, Ii)

Subject: The First of a Hundred Copies?

I came across a Kate Bush topic on GEnie the other day.  As you would
expect, it's populated by typical group of enthusiastic KT fans but
not having access to Love-Hounds they're a little short on information
(they knew about the boxed set but not the convention).  Would anyone
object if I occaisionally forward important posts to this topic on
GEnie.  Some people in other newsgroups have objected to posting
USENET information to a for-profit network like GEnie.

	[ Go right ahead.  I don't care whether anyone makes a profit
          off of this or not -- |>oug ]
--

It appears that Love-Hounds may have gone abruptly silent in the wake of
that ridiculous blast of noise.  Did someone pull the plug until
everything gets under control or is everyone just afraid to post for
fear that it will appear 100 or so times?

	[Yes, I am moderating Love-Hounds by hand, at the moment.
	 -- |>oug ]

--

I wonder with all of these Love-Hounds going to represent us at the
convention if we might be able to work out some sort of group rates
for _Homeground_ subscriptions or Kate Bush Club memberships.  I'm not
really even looking for a discount, just some way of getting around
the currency conversion that keeps giving me an excuse to procrastinate. 

--

It sure is nice to have so much to look forward to so soon after the
release of _The Sensual World_.  The boxed set may not represent
anything new but it will mean that we can finally enjoy some of Kate's
finest work -- the B-sides -- without concern for rapidly
deteriorating vinyl or tape media.

If you haven't heard the B-sides you're in for a real treat.
