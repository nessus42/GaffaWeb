 >>...if only they'd quit fooling with this 60's sound. 
 >
 >... the 60's sound is something they love.  Why should they turn their
 >backs on it?  Are they really trying to please their fans?  I think
 >they are trying to please themselves.


	I'm not objecting to the 60's sound, really. 


		And I'm not saying XTC are trying to please the fans.
	What I'm saying is that the *producer* is trying to please
	the fans. He's created some very, very... *accessible* (Ick!)
	This band deserves batter than to be watered down and prettied
	up. They have more fire and emotion than that, and it's been 
	badly dampered by this limp-wristed production.


		*Note*: I'm not saying the record is badly recorded.
	It's not. It's just that the man at the helm had no idea who
	and what he was working with. It's like Elton John's 'Goodbye
	Yellow Brick Roead'; some great songs, ruined by a producer 
	who insisted on mixing the guitars down, adding strings, and
	fading everything out....



 colm@mathcs.emory.edu (Colm Mulcahy) Writes:

>it's funny, Andy said that of the 36 songs he submitted to Rundgren, a lot were
