He never thought it would come to this, but
in spite of his sincere sympathy for the
insulted party (Sue), IED feels that it
would be a bad move to pressure Wicinski
to leave. If popularity were grounds for
removal from Love-Hounds, IED would not
care to guess where he would be now. Certainly
Wicinski is a foul-mouthed, mean-spirited
jerkwad, and his remarks about Sue were
totally unjustified. But he does add life
to the L-H proceedings, and his use of language
occasionally bears some modicum of low, subcultural
aesthetic worth. IED enjoins Sue to reconsider, and
fight fire with fire... Fire has always been an effective
method of fumigation.

-- Andrew Marvick
