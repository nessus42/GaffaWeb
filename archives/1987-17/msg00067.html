with either _White_Music_ or _Go2_, this lyric sheet acts as a catch-all

There is no mention of "Take This Town" (which I have only ever found
on the soundtrack of "Times Square"), so I assume it came after
_Drums_And_Wires_.

NOW . . .

My _Drums_And_Wires_ has "Life Begins At the Hop" in the place of "Day 
In Day Out", and the jacket lists it that way.  My copy did *not* have 
the free single stuffed into it.

Then I got the CD of _Drums_And_Wires_.  It lists the same songs that
the album lists (LBATH instead of DIDO) on the booklet and on the
case and on the CD itself.  However, there are fourteen tracks instead
of twelve.  "Day In Day Out" appears where "Life Begins At the Hop" is
listed to be; "Chain Of Command" appears before "Real By Reel"; and
"Limelight" follows "Complicated Game".

Also, every copy of "Go2" that I have ever seen has "Are You
Receiving Me?" as the last or next-to-last track on side one.
In fact, my "Go2" CD has "Are You Receiving Me?" and "This Is Pop"
on it.

And every copy I've ever seen of _White_Music_ has "Statue Of Liberty"
included on it.

You go and figure it out.

BTW, I just got _Chips_From_the_Chocolate_Fireball_ today.  I notice
that they decided to go ahead and do the Beach Boys on _Psonic_
Psunspot_ better than they did on "Season Cycle".  _Chips_ is a
*great* CD, and I am elated to have better-sounding versions of the
songs from _25_O'Clock_.

Also, an old Andy Partridge album seems to be making the import rounds
once again.  It is titled _Mr._Partridge_'Take_Away'_.  Side One is
called "Take Away", and Side Two is called "The Lure Of Salvage".
To quote the liner notes: "This used to be some XTC records.  It is 
now a collection of tracks that have been electronically processed/
shattered and layered with other sounds or lyrical pieces.  If you liked
'Go+', then this record weighs approximately the same amount."
Recommended for *hard-core* XTC fans *only*.  There's some really weird
stuff on this album.
