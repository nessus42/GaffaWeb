Last week I posted a message regarding a possible lead on the identity
of the "Crippen" mentioned in "Coffee Homeground."  The lead consisted
of a book reference and statement of its availability at several of
the libraries in the UC system.  I've since found a reference for the
book in the Cornell Law Library, which I'll attempt to follow up.
Just in case anyone else is ambitious, I'm here including the complete
catalog entry and call numbers for the libraries that I know include
it in their holdings.  I'd appreciate an acknowledgement from anyone
willing to help me check this out.

Author:		Crippen, Hawley Harvey, 1862-1910, defendant.
Title:		The trial of Hawley Harvey Crippen, ed. with notes and
		  an introduction by Filson Young ... Edinburgh and
		  London, W. Hodge & Company, ltd. [1920].
Description:	xxxv, 211 p. 5 port. (incl. front.) facsim. 23 cm.
Series:		Notable English trials.
Notes:		Dr. Crippen was tried in 1910, for the murder of his
		  wife, Cora Crippen.

UCLA Law Lib TR N8433 C868
CSL Main Lib K645.H6 C931 Law Library
UCI Main Lib KD373.C75 1950
UCB Law Lib KD373.C75 Y68
HAST Library KD373.C863
UCD Law Lib KD373.C75 Y68 1950
UCSF Library KD373.C7 1950 History
Cornell Law Library - Trials KD370.N8 C93 1950
Book is not listed in library catalogs for Princeton, Dartmouth,
University of Michigan, University of Virginia, Columbia.

I refuse to enter into silly "discussions" of how I discovered Kate
(some people know already anyway) or which album is best/least good.
I listen to them as the mood strikes me, about equally often.  I do
have favorite songs, but those change too.  Actually, my most recent
frequent listen is one titled "Jon's Faves and B-sides" (thanks ever
so much, Jon!) which I'm almost always in the mood for.  "Under the
Ivy," "Be Kind to My Mistakes," "Walk Straight Down the Middle," "The
Big Sky" mix, "The Sensual World," "Ne T'Enfuis Pas," "Ken" all on the
same tape!

Jenn
