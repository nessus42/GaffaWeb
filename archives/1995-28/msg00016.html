It's not that simple. The guy is using no less than four different 
accounts on fur different systems. He forges the return addresses on all 
his posts so that the system that sent the message is never the one 
that's supposed to get the replies.

This guy is a well-known and notorious spammer, and has been kicked off of
systems before -- he always finds a way back on. Part of the key to his
success is that he doesn't *care* if you mailbomb him back. A good portion
of the mail goes to the wrong -- forged -- addresses, and what doesn't he
probably has directed straight to /dev/null, or he is perfectly willing to
sacrifice the account, since he can always find another. His system
kjl.com is an MX-only site, it doesn't have an address. That means it's
just a SLIP/PPP account or a UUCP node. I figure he never really checks
the mail there at all, just has his provider delete it on a regular basis. 

He has been confronted about the spamming before and has the audacity to
claim he knows nothing about it. Furthermore, nothing he is doing is
definitely "illegal" in the purely judicial sense of the word, it's just
terrible nettiquette. Criminal law just isn't ready for the internet yet. 

Our only reasonable recourse is to go after the sysadmins of the various 
sites he is using, chief of which is freenet.fsu.edu, which doesn't seem 
to have a clue or give a damn.
