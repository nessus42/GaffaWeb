Believe or not, Chris & I agree with you. As a matter of fact, we'll
go even further and do something you very kindly didn't do-blame Kate.
Let it be known that this is one of our all time favorite Kate songs.
On the Castaway soundtrack, it's THE perfect pop song. On the Castaway
soundtrack, it's one of the most perfect songs ever written by anybody,
anytime, anywhere. On the Castaway soundtrack, it rates as THE MOST
UNDERRATED SONG OF THE MILLEMIUM. On the Castaway soundtrack, this
song deserved to be a number one hit in every country in the known
universe for, oh, at least a year (if not longer).

              What the world needed:

     ** A 20 MINUTE SUPER-DUPER EXTRA-LONG EXTENDED MIX **

              What the world got:

           -- a shortened version --

  AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAARRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRGGGGGGGGGGGGGGGGGGGG!!!!!!!!!!!!!

I can't even BEGIN to imagine what Kate was thinking of when she
butchered it the way she did. Yeah, it's nice to have the song on
CD, but the changes she made hurt us to the core of our being so
it's certainly no fun to listen to therefore we've only listened
to it twice. I guess I really envy people who've never heard the
original. To them, it must sound fine. Don't anybody point out 
that she only cut out a few seconds. I don't care if she cut out
two seconds or twenty or one hundred & twenty. The point is, the
song didn't deserve any sort of axe job at all!!  For Hounds she
gave us lots of extended mixes, now she's shortening songs-what??
For us (and no one should for a second doubt our high level of
"worship"), it's THE most disappointing thing she's ever done in
her entire career. 


{{{ Chris here;

   The major problem with the shortened version of _Be Kind to My Mistakes_,
IMHO, is that the best part of the song is cut out. Kate has complete control  
over her music and generally this is good, but her control, combined with
her working pace, keeps the musicians from being able to explore "a groove"
in the time honered fashion. Rarely does more than one musician at a time
play at Kate's sessions. This assures us of getting Kate's vision (slo-o-wly)
but prevents us from hearing the sort of magic that can happen when great
players can work out a melodic and rhythmic "groove". _Be Kind to My Mistakes_
(the long version) is one of the few examples of this in Kate's work. Others
that spring quickly to mind are the Irish musicians in _Night of the Swallow_
and _Jig of Life_; I can't imagine that _The Chieftains_ could work at their
best ability in a multi-track format, or that the _Trio Bulgarka_ could sing
their parts separately. This disembowling of _Be Kind to My Mistakes_ is 
nothing short of heartbreaking. This is a good example of why Kate _MUST_
tour. In a tour situation the players can stretch out and play to their full
ability (not to say that there isn't some brilliant players and playing on
Kate's records, just that such things take a back seat to Kate's vision).}}}
