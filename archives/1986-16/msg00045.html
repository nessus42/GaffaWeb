	Way back when ...
	pre 1972: single(s?) released.. forgotten the names, but redone
		  on I think  "NEU 2" (second albumn .. you guessed it)
	1972: (from memory.. )first albumn released: "HALLOGALLO"..
		  .. included fantastic heavy synth/rock/spacey/industrial
		  track called "Negativeland".. check the heavy drilling
		  sounds at the intro.. when "EISTURZENDE.." were still shaking
		  rattlers.
	1973: Second Albumn : "NEU 2".. this time very much working with 
		  recordings reused at various speeds and the like..
		  interesting, but perhaps a little "overdone(?)"...
		  side 1 starting to sound very much like LA DUSSELDORF.
	****: I know of at least one other self titled albumn which a friend
		  owns (I've got the above two..), and I do seem to recall
		  "NEU 75" from somewhere.

	( all of course in association with Conny Planck.. )

	Rother/Dinger also ~'73 started up a concurrent project under the 
	name LA DUSSELDORF. The albumn "VIVA" is an absolute must if your 
	at all into the (now almost extinct) big german "synth" sound.
	..Quick to add, not to be confused with the sort of melancholic 
	boredom that KLAUS SHULZE has been giga-overated for...
	Side two of VIVA has a great "classical/repetitive" track called
	"CHA-CHA 2000". I'm fairly sure nothing NEUw has been done under the 
	psychonim "NEU" since the mid seventies though. Later collections
	of german musics (BLACK GAT., HARMONIA etc.) were from memory just
	revamps as far as NEU is concerned. 
	... "but baby that was years ago.."
