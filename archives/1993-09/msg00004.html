In article <C1yHML.M4J@chinet.chi.il.us> jorn@chinet.chi.il.us (Jorn Barger) writes:
>so if anyone wants to email me a brief explanation of how to delete lines
>easily in whatever editors they use most, i'll see if this idea is practical...

Well, the easiest way in (gnu)emacs is to select a range, and delete
it.  With the standard keymapping, set the beginning of the range with
control-space.  Move to the end of the range, and delete with ^W.  The
range is put into the kill buffer, so you can later yank it back (^Y).
