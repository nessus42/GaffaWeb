> Inspired.  Overinspired, but inspired.
> For one thing, there are *no* loose, healthy characters in FULL METAL
> JACKET who still remember how to shimmy.  Everybody in that film,
> including the Matthew Modine character, is tainted and corrupted by the
> war.  Modine does actively struggle against that, most notably by his
> sarcasm and his peace button, but he also ultimately buys into the system.
> Any shimmying elements (Nancy Sinatra, the Surfing Bird, the Mickey Mouse
> theme) are subverted towards the war.
> 
> Also, in the one scene where psychoanalytic paradigms are drawn, Modine
> specifically invokes Jung, not Reich.
> 
> And you know, the more I think about it, name any character in any
> Stanley Kubrick film loose and healthy enough to remember how to shimmy.

> on the videophone in 2001, and she's only there for a minute or two.
> 
> Full Metal Jacket, btw, means that one is carrying all of one's
> ammunition, in that over the shoulder style war films fetishize.
> But the film isn't so much about the fascism of that pose, but
> the absurdity of it, and of war.  If it draws upon any school of
> thought, I'd have to say it draws upon absurdism.
> 
> Which is not to suggest that there is nothing in what Jorn says;

FYI, to clear up a incorrect definition, "Full Metal Jacket" is
the type of ammo used by the US ARMY for rifles and machine guns.
Vincent D'Nofrio (sp), the harrassed private in boot says as much
while loading his rifle just before shooting the drill sergeant.

Being an ex-Army person, myself, in Soldier of the Year Competitions,
I know this by rote, ie- 
	Q: What is the M-16 rifle characteristics?
	A: The M-16 rifle is a semi automatic or automatic
	   rifle. It has an effective range of 300m...etc...
	   It fires a 5.56mm _full metal jacket_ round...

Now that important fact is cleared (like somebody _really_ gives a s**t
that I pounced on this...the important question...

Two and a half years ago, while on a trip to SF, I ventured into
a record shop.  They also sold autographed albums and other asst.
musical stuff.  

Was shown a copy of "The Sensual World", signed by Herself.  I was proud
of myself for thinking hard about three minutes (really it was delaying
about three minutes) before purchasing it.  It had just arrived in the
store that morning and I did not want to risk losing it to another
buyer if I thought about it longer.

My question is, are there other people out there with autographed
albums (my was framed already) of Kate or any musician that can tell
me if this is a valid and valuable item and how to verify that
	a) It is REAL (It really LOOKS like Kates sig)
	b) what the usual price is
	c) and if the values on these ever rise...
I genuinely hope that this causes some people out there pea-green
envy! :-)

Thanks for any info that can be given.....
