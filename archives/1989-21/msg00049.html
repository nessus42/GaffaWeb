No, but you're close!  Kate carelessly mistranscribed the town name "Gafsa"
as "Gaffa".  Gafsa, I'm sure you're aware, is a city (mining center and
oasis) in west-central Tunisia (pop. 24,000).  Now the true meaning of the
song can be told.  Kate, _obviously_, traveled to Gafsa to do some consulting
work for the mines, but couldn't leave after she finished her work because of
some (unspecified) problem with her passport or visa--thereby leaving her
"Suspended in Gaffa [sic]".  Oh, and by the way, "Out in the garden there is
half of heaven" doesn't refer to a 16 track tape recorder--it refers to the
famous "Half of Heaven" ruins of Gafsa (a major tourist attraction), which
she could see from her hotel room.  :-)
