Not sure what the song is about, but the verse you posted has the phrase
"bean in Eirinn" three times, which means "lady of Ireland." So Obviously
it's about a girl. 

"beo" means alive, lively or quick.

"fhearr" is a feminine of Fearr, which means better or best.
"bhinne"is fem of binne, sweet-sounding or melodious.
"Ceoil" is music. So the second line has something to do with a beautiful
song, I guess, sung by or about said Irish lady.

The rest of it, well, you got me.
