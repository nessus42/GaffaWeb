> Well, from what the Kate Bush: Visual Documentary sez (Omnibus, 1990)
> Del was the original bassist in Kate's older brother's band, which was
> yer average pub-rock 70's thing with Celtic influences. Kate became the
> band's default singer while still in her teens, playing local Kent pubs
> like the Hope and Anchor. On her original EMI-Harvest recordings her
> producers insisted on replacing her "live" band with well-known studio
> musicians from bands like Pilot. Later, as she took more control over her
> own work, she brought in the original band (Del was by this time her
> boyfriend). Palmer produced her 1990 album The Sensual World. The couple
> split up sometime around the recording of the Red Shoes album.
> Palmer's been photographed with a JayDee bass: I don't know if he ever
> had a Wal.
> Other fretless players who've guested on Kate's albums include Mick Karn
> (formerly of Japan) and John Giblin (Simple Minds, Brand X), who use
> Wal/Klein and Zon basses, respectively.
