Subject: Harmonizers, etc.

Excuse me, but the thing that Laurie ANderson runs her voice through 
most these days is called a Harmonizer, and it's made by Eventide 
CLockworks.  The vocoder superimposes the formant frequencies of 
human (or kanga) speech on some other signal, and the Harmonizer 
simply allows one to shift the frequency of the incoming signal by 
something approaching an octave and a half in either direction. Peter 
Gabriel used one when he was touring live after the first record. 
He'd use it to sing this little song to his teddy bear sitting on the 
piano. The tune showed up on his second album, thought the performance 
I saw featured wordless vocals.  [Kate Bush also used one to do the
"Satanic" vocals in "Waking The Witch". -- Doug]
