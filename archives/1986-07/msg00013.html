>I think FNX's p.o.v. is that you shouldn't be listening to any other station

>>This is not to say I think "alternative stations" can play

>>...But still,  I think that being "snobby" is really only

>
>I agree. But 'FNX sells itself as "Rock the Boat Radio", an alternative to
>hearing the same "drivel" over and over again. They're not being snobby,
>they're simply playing one type of music. Does that mean WDLW is snobby
>because it only plays C&W?

But the fact of the matter is that FNX just plays *different* drivel over
and over again.  Example:  About four months ago I had heard Camper Van
Beethoven on WFMU (just outside NYC).  When I got back to Boston, I
called FNX and requested "Take the Skinheads Bowling" by CVB.  Not only
did the DJ not play it, the guy who took my call was snotty and
condescending.  My guess is that since the Boston Phoenix hadn't
reviewed it yet (have they by *now*?) and since nobody in the band had
been in the "new wave" scene c.1980 (e.g., Fine Audio Cannabals, Big
Young Dynamite, General Difford & Tilbrook Public), they weren't worth a
serious listen.  So what *really* has happened is that there is a new
pack of critical and airplay favorites.  No "ALTERNATIVE".
