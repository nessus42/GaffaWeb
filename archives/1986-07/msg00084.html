Pete Alfke pens:

>Has anyone noticed that Brian Eno's "King's Lead Hat" and the Cars'
>"Shake It Up" have the exact same chord progressions in the verses?

I never noticed it until I played KLH for my mainstream-pop buddies in
high school to try and get them out of their shells. The only comment
was "It sounds like Shake It Up." I said "Blah!" and took my record
home, never to attempt to broaden their tiny heads again... never took
it serious, tho!

{} {} {} {} {} {} {} {} {} {}

Well. The first mention of "Sledgehammer" has appeared in the current
New Musical Depressed. Here it is, in all it's ugly glory...

PETER GABRIEL: Sledgehammer
(Virgin)
"Open up your   fruit-cage,"    sings
Peter,  "I    wanna be  your   honey-
bee."  Something  is   up   in Pete's
world    of  silly  monkey    make-up
and  arch    gestures.  He  has  been
played  a Phil   Collins record.  And
he   has  watched   amazed   as   his
little bearded  chum,    the   cheery
chappy    drummer    from     halcyon
days of sell-out   college gigs,  has
turned  into  every  Renault   Five's
cassette     pet.      `Sledgehammer'
follows  Collins'  production    line
"soul" struts, is intercut with brass
and  backing    vocals,   and    even
Gabriel's   whine  is        tailored
towards a Hall & Oates  cut. I  fully
expect   to  see   Gabriel on  Top of
The    Pops,   wearing   a  cool blue
suit, RayBans,   and   a skinny  tie.
And      wearing     bloody    stupid
monkey make-up.

		- David Quantick

Oh blah. It's still selling well, at least.
