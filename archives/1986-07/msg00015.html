Actually from your above description the two sound very similar. i.e. one
geek music director gets hundreds of releases each week, reads Billboard
and maybe NME - finds from the major label outlets what he should be pushing
(in order to keep them record ads a'coming) - chooses 5 or 6 records, pockets
the money from selling the rest (or maybe buys a couple "hot" import singles).
Most of the music directors (as in college) are obnoxious geeks interested
only finding out how to make money off of music.  (They call it "playing
the game")  In the case of Camper Van Beetoveen, get them 
a release on CBS, and the right attitude (i.e. sound spiteful towards their
label while sucking the nipple) , and a couple of haircuts and t-shirts
and FNX and HFS will be slobbering over the vinyl!  (Wait!  CVB already has
an attitude!)  The few indies they do play are usually unoffensive drivel
played in the name of tokenism, I mean I listened to HFS back in /83 when 
bands like Circle Jerks were getting airplay only because they made a medly of 
ridiculous top 40 sendups.

Ya' see, Penn, CVB released on an indie
label - one which don't have the $GELDER$ to play the corporate commercial radio
game.  Doug has heard HFS in its older days (bethesda) when they had some DJ's
(J. Walker, Mitch PArker, Milo) who really cared about music, so much that they
spent their salarys on buying releases that the director had locked up or sold
off in order to play a representive sample of "new" music.  Also, HFS will play
some new stuff late at night - usually where they put the DJ's who don't tow
the station manager's line, anyway.

They're as much as the establishment as they don't want you to believe they 
aren't. There's usually some sort of inverse rule there when dealing with 
commercial entities I think.  When they do play an indie it's only after the hip
 have  truly nodded and the major labels are making serious advances towards the
 band or as a gesture of tokenism.  

The bottom line is money and always will be money.

wattage so it can reach from Phillie up to Boston.
