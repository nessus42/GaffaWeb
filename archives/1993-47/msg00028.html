In article <Pine.3.05.9311181425.F9446-b100000@atlas.cs.upei.ca> you write:
>First off, a note of thanks to Tom Estes for circulating his "video 
>compilation" of Kate stuff. I watched the tape last night and it is
>truly excellent. Even if everybody on gaffa likes to flame each other
>on a regular basis, I think it speaks for the strength of the group
>that something like this can be circulated on  a (relatively) cooperative
>basis. And, Tom, I hope your VCR isn't worn out from it :)

   Credit must go to Jorn Barger; the tape tree was his idea - he put
the people together. It was a great idea, and remained one after he
flamed-out. Especial thanks should go to Peter Manchester - without
whom nothing would have happened.

>Now a question. According to Kate Bush Complete, there are two different
>versions of the Wuthering Heights video. One is the studio one we all
>know and love, with the famous fluttering white dress. On this compilation
>tape, there is the one shot in the Gothic amusement park (which is
>terrific! ). But the (third?) one mentioned is "an outdoor one shot
>by Rockflix" which was only distributed abroad. Is this the same as
>the Gothic amusement park one? Can anyone clarify?

   No, the Rockflix video has Kate in a red dress and boots, tromping
around a patch of ground that passes for the moors. The Dutch videos
were not used for promotional purposes other than the Dutch TV
broadcast. The Rockflix video was used in the UK early, until the 
Keefco video was made. The Rockflix video was used in Austraila.
