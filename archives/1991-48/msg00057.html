Vickie here. I'm glad to see other Toni Childs fans here too. I liked
_Union_ a lot (my favorite song is "Zimbabwe") but _House of Hope_ is
even better, IMHO. It's very dark, very sad and very powerful lyrically,
yet musically, it's hard to stay still while listening to it. It's not 
a dance album, more like a slither and glide album. "Daddy's Song" ranks
as one of the most heart-wrenchingly powerful songs I've ever heard. It's
about incest and it's not for the faint of heart. Angry...and chilling.
"I gave you all my love...and you showed no mercy" Ohmygod. I don't know
very much about Toni Childs, but I remember reading that she ran away
from home at a very early age, 15 or 16, and that she's had a very rough
life. Definitely a survivor-type, a very strong woman and extremely
talented. I've never seen her in concert (or even a video) but I would 
like to. I envy those who have. 

btw, Laurie Freelove *is* the same one who used to be in Two Nice Girls.

or 405. Our news feed is down, so I've missed out on a lot, I think. Any
kind soul willing to send these to me? Thanks in advance.
