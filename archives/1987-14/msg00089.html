KT News:

Still more Kate Bush bootleg LPs have appeared on the market. The
first one features a Hounds of Love-type cover set against a photograph
of pinkish satin -- it includes, if IED recalls correctly, the Paris
'79 concert tape again.
     The second is a new two-record set, with a close-up of the
photo used for the U.S. Hounds of Love LP poster (Kate kneeling
with a Turkish fez on her head). This is certainly the most
brazen and daring KT bootleg to appear to date. It includes
bad second-generation transfers of many official 12" remixes and b-sides,
plus several well-known but not officially released tracks, such
as the 1980 live version of "Let It Be", etc. It's actually a very
nice collection of stuff, but as Homeground recently said of a
European KT bootleg that has surfaced over there, "the contents
invite criminal prosecution."

-- Andrew
