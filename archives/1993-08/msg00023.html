REGARDING                KBOO, Kate, and Bolero
people:

Portland, Oregon LoveHounders might be interested in checking out KBOO (90.7
on your FM dial) Friday mornings betwixt 8 am (perhaps sooner) and 9 am.
Somone's running a show featuring exclusively women musical artists and
hasn't yet failed to play Kate (tho' I only discovered it a few weeks back
so have a very small sample size). The rest of the content is quite
outstanding anyway so who cares if Kate doesn't appear for a few weeks or so
(gosh, did I actually write that?). 

Maybe all Friday is women's day on KBOO. At least it was today. In the
afternoon they played the new Four Bitchin' Babes CD. This is probably not a
LoveHounders usual fair but you all might give them a listen. A fine mixture
of humourous and beautiful folk. If you like Christine Lavine (the groups
nominal organizer) you will certainly like the other 3 (names learned last
summer and quickly forgotten but they tend to change anyway).

Who says there are no words to Bolero? There are some fine words provided by
one Albert Brooks on an old album (another name forgotten) on which he
provided a song for every type of radio station in the country. A get rich
scheme that didn't make it. Anyway the words are marvelous but will forever
destroy any appreciation you may have had of the tune in it's original
musical state. Seek at your own risk.

After all these years I've finally read Love Hounds to the music of Kate
Bush. An experience not to be passed by lightly...

where angels fear to tread,
