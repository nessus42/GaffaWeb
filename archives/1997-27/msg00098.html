Gee, from the sound of it a lot of you have had some trouble introducing 
KaTe to friends. I, on the other hand, have done quite well in this. I 
suppose it's because I "plan my attack" carefully. My music collection is 
wide and varied. I can usually find *something* in it that others like. 
So what I normally do is basically have a good-old late nighter 
music-a-thon.

I get the victu... err.. prospective new KaTe fan :-) to bring over their 
music and we sort of swap songs and talk. If they can tell me an 
interesting story about a particular piece of music I then might quickly 
do the same with a KaTe song. It's important to really listen to what 
they have to say about their music (hey, I've discovered lots of cool 
stuff this way) I find it easy to get enthusiastic right along with them.

Another thing to do is take your cues from them. If their interest wavers 
then it's time to move on or maybe tell them more about the music as it 
plays. KaTe has a wide enough range of songs and styles to find 
*something* that a new listener will find non-threatening.

If, by the end of the night, I haven't converted a new KaTe fan, I have 
at least introduced them to her and they leave with a greater 
understanding and at least (I hope) some respect for her and those fans 
who love her.
