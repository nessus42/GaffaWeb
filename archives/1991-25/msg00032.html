    3.	For all you party hosts here is a recipe for Seedcake:

	Take all ingredients out of the refrigerator and let them sit
	until at room temp.
	Preheat oven to 375.
	Sift		1 teaspoon salt
			4 teaspoon baking powder
	into		4 cup flour.
	Cream 		1.5 cup butter.
	Add and continue creaming until light
			3 cup sugar.
	Add		8 eggs
	one at a time and continue beating after each egg.
	Slowly add in the flour mixture and
			1 cup milk
			2 teaspoon vanilla
	mixing the whole time.
	Finally (and this is what makes this ordinary pound cake a seed cake)
	add		1 teaspoon poppy seed.
			(Maybe it's supposed to be 1 tablespoon.  Oh well,
			 start with a teaspoon, and if that doesn't look
			 like enough, add two more teaspoons.)
	
	Pour into two 9x9 pans or two 4.5x8 loaf pans.  I plan on using
	cup cake tins myself.  If using the loaf pans you can line with
	parchment, leaving excess out the sides, before pouring batter 
	in so that removal is easier.  If desired you can lightly dust the 
	final product with confectioner's sugar.
