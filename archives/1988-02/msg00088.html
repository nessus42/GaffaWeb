Hmmm. I have TKI, HoL, Lionheart on the imported label (EMI) while my
copies of NFE and The Dreaming are on the domestic (EMI America)
label. Both domestic CD's have a very good sound quality. I was
concerned about both at purchase time but I ended up pleasantly
suprised. Of the imports, TKI exhibits some hiss but the clarity in
the vocals leads me to believe that this is more a problem of the
recording equipment in that era than a problem with using an inferior
copy of the original master tape. TKI on EMI is still very good.  When
I say there is some hiss I am being somewhat picky.

While I haven't heard the domestic Lionheart CD I can say the the
import is excellent. It is at least as good as the sound quality of
the domestic copy of NFE that I have (which by the rating above would
put it at a "9 or better") This is easy for me to remember since this
is my favorite Kate CD and I rarely get out of the house in the
morning without listening to at least 5 songs off of that CD.  (I
notice that most people here seem to favor The Dreaming. Am I the only
one picking Lionheart above her other works?)
