Regarding the line "Now is the place where the crossroads meet":

>> [Me:] That line means "Now is an important moment in time because you
>> are either going to drown or not, and it's up to you which one."

> [jer:] But, that would be a fork in the road, like in the Robert Frost
> poem, not a "crossroads".

It depends on how you look at time.  One might look at it the way Roger
Waters does:

	They flutter behind you, your possible pasts
	Some bright-eyed and crazy, some frightened and lost
	A warning to anyone still in command
	Of their possible futures, to take care

Besides, "the crossroads of time" is a common expression.

			-Doug
