hi!  it's just GIF mania up there on hayes.ims.alaska.edu.  i've
taken the liberty of adding a few more pictures to the collection,
all scanned from the original tour program i picked up at the KonvenTion.

the files are greyscale GIF format, with no more than 128 colors.
i used Adobe Photoshop on the Mac to clean the scans up -- nonetheless
there are still a few artifacts due to the glossy/bumpy texture
ot the program.

i have scanned every page of the program booklet, plus the enclosing
folder and a page of the stationary.  sadly, my program is lacking the
poster included in the original package...

the pages marked * are photos of KaTe (and generally of high quality);
those not so marked are text, pictures of the band, etc, and are
of more scholarly interest...  i wish i could have gotten the pastel
artwork on the folder (folder.gif) in color -- it's really gorgeous.

these GIFs (and this message) are in the directory:

	pub/love-hounds/kb/pix/gif_tour_program


* folder.gif		-- pastel artwork of a faun-like KaTe face.  big.
  folder_inside_l.gif	-- list of concert dates
* folder_inside_r.gif	-- lionhearty portrait

  cover.gif		-- same as folder.gif plus handwritten message
* back_cover.gif	-- slightly odd portrait (a bit blurred)

  stationary.gif	-- was lilac!  similar to folder.gif + photo

* page_1.gif	-- black leotard photo
  page_2.gif	-- act one blurb
* page_3.gif	-- amazing portrait
  page_4.gif	-- act two blurb
* page_5.gif	-- wuthering heights/lace portrait (with some of the border)
  page_6.gif	-- act three blurb
  page_7.gif	-- band portraits (inc. Paddy and Del)
  page_8.gif	-- band portraits (inc. the dancers and Simon Drake)
  page_9.gif	-- credits
  page_10.gif	-- adverts and credits

* lionheart.gif	-- the logo from page_9

i hope to write up a detailed description of the program to go with 
these pictures -- i'll place in the same directory when it's done.
any interesting facts or trivia about the program would be appreciated!

feel free to comment on the worth of these GIFs -- pages 9 and
10, for example, could be summarized in far less space than the 
GIF files take...
