On smoking and aging affecting high vocal range:

	If vocal technique is correct, smoking will do far more damage than
aging.  Witness Luciano Pavarotti--he's in his 50's or 60's, but he still
warms up to high F'' (second F above middle C).  Tell me again about aging
destroying a voice!  Incorrect use over time, maybe, but not aging by itself.
I suggest McCloskey's book (can't remember the title), in which a man in his
70's regained his high tenor simply by learning correct technique.
