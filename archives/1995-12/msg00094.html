That's three mentions for one item! At least, I think so. What Ray
Cokes showed the European audience was the official EMI promo box
I held in my hands at a record fair in Amsterdam back in October
1993. For the contents I quote a piece from the UK "Record
Collector" magazine, published October 1993:
 
-------------------------
"THE RED SHOES" PROMO BOX
 
Sure to form part of any future rarities listing is the promotional
shoebox designed to whet critics' appetites for Kate Bush's lastest
album.
This 1,000-only collectible, which is shoebox shaped (size 4-and-a-
half, of course, as per Kate's feet), is still being manifactured
as we go to press, but word has it that it will include a special
fountain pen; a video, which will probably feature the "Rubberband
Girl" excerpt from the "The Red Shoes"  film, A CD of the album;
and a biography printed on parchment paper, neatly tied with red
ribbon.
-------------------------
 
Note that...
...I don't know what a "fountain pen" is, but it was black, and
tied with a red ribbon.
...I didn't see the video, but it could very well contain both the
RBG clip and the Aspel performance, I vaguely recall the dealer
mentioning something like that.
...Next to the things mentioned above, there was also a slide
contained in the box, with a picture of Kate dressed in a black
dress, and, of course, the small red shoes Ray Cokes tried on as
earrings... :)
 
I didn't buy this beauty of a box, due to the going rate of 400
Guilders, some $225. It will undoubtedly be worth even more now.
 
Hope this info clears up some Red Shoe Box confusion.
