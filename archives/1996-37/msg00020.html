Dear me, what an interesting question!  All these years I've had to
struggle against the misconception of others that I liked Kate's work just
because I thought she was sexy, with the offensive corollary that a female
artist can or should be judged by how sexy she looks in a photograph.  But
obviously, she is an attractive person!

My favorite photos of Kate are never the carefully composed, heavily
made-up studio shots, but as that sort of photo goes, Jay Bush is a hell of
a lot more tasteful and creative than the typical publicity photographer.
I always prefer more spontaneous candid shots:

1)  The cover of the July 1985 issue of Keyboard magazine.  It's available
at:  http://www.gryphon.com/gaffa/wow/K309.jpg with an alternate shot,
K310.jpg, that is equally good.  This is the first thing I think of when I
envision Kate; happily sitting at her Fairlight, ready to work.  Not at all
the fierce otherworldly creature in some of the publicity photos.

2)  The December 1993 issue of Q Magazine, page 102.  It's available at:
http://www.gryphon.com/gaffa/wow/K334.jpg -- Kate wearing what appears to
be a black leather coat over a heavy gray turtleneck.  Her arms are folded
and her expression seems frankly surly.  It looks like it's very cold out.
I gather some other people find this picture unappealing, but to me she
looks more ravishing than ever.

3)  One of the "Cathy" photos by Jay Bush; I don't have the book so I can't
identify this one better, but it's available at:
http://www.gryphon.com/gaffa/wow/K136.jpg -- Kate has a very distinctive
face at any age, but it's frankly amazing that her adult appearance comes
through so strongly at such an early age.

I recommend a browse through the "Tiny Thumbnails" of Vickie's Wow section
in Gaffaweb to all before compiling your lists; it's very instructive!
