From: marsal1!iemisi.smartin@uunet.UU.NET (Stephen Martin)

> Here is a question concerning a Kate Bush album that i saw in a used record
> store here in Toronto this weekend.  It was a three album set called, i
> believe _Kate_Bush_Live_in_Europe_  the cover had two nice early pictures of
> Kate and the price was $60.  I have never seen this album before and have not
> seen it listed in any discographys.  Is it rare, is it worth forking over the
> big bucks for??

It's a bootleg containing moderately good quality copies of the "Live
at Hammersmith Odeon" video soundtrack, the German-TV "Kate!" special and
an unidentified concert (paris, I believe).

DO NOT PAY $60 FOR THIS RECORD.

I bought it at Second Coming Records in Cambridge, a place I absolutely
revile as the most overpriced store in the universe after the chains.
It was only $21.  It may just be worth $21, but no way in hell is it
worth $60.  Unless you are absolutely desparate for some poorly mastered
bootlegs, that is.  If you're feeling frisky, rent a HiFi VCR, a copy
of the Hammersmith Odeon tape, and tape it on a decent home tape deck
(use a chrome tape, of course!)... This will probably cost less than $10,
and is definitely the better buy.

Or rent a laserdisk player and the laserdisk of it, assuming you can
find a place with both of those items...

Avoid the bootleg unless you can find it for a reasonable price and
really like scratchy hissy stuff.
