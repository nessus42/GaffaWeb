>you live in Suffolk, you migt try WRCN 103.9, they play everything

When I wrote that I couldn't listen to WDRE for too long, it was due
to a combination of inane commercials and a restricted repertoire
(i.e., a limited range of tempoes, etc.).  A mix of new music and
classic rock . . . sounds like heaven, or CHOM-FM in Montreal!  (To be
fair, I switch between Q107.1 and CFNY 102.3 in Toronto, and get lots
of both.)

In New York City, WNEW 102.7 dabbles into a lot of stuff along the
outside edges of the "classic rock" domain -- right now, they're
playing the new Midge Ure single "Dear God."  It looks like they find
anyone who's participated in rock charity (e.g., FreedomFest,
Live-Aid, etc.) legit, although they don't touch Marillion.  They had
side I of "Hounds of Love" as one of their "Midnight Perfect Album
Sides" not too long ago.

To be fair, WNEW's been spending more time hawking ex-Allman Bro.
Dickie Betts new stuff than all their new music time combined...
