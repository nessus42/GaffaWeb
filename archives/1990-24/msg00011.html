 Vickie here. I've been a bit out of touch recently but now I have news:

                         ! FINALLY !

Received a phone call from Peter David Fitgerald-Morris yesterday
and he gave me the lowdown on the convention and some info on the
Box Set. 

First the convention.

When: Saturday, November 17 1990
      11:00am-7:00pm

Where: La Palais, Peter didn't have the exact address with him but it's
       located in the Hammersmith district of West London. He said he
       thought that it was on the same street as the Odeon but don't 
       quote him on that.
*** (Can any of our English friends tell us about this district. What's
it like? Is it easy to get to? Anything at all.) ***
       

The tickets will be priced at 5 pounds. Those "off the isle" should
send British cash or stirling to:

        Homeground
        P.O. Box 176
        Orpington, Kent U.K.  BR5 3NA

He didn't specify anything else about the cost except that all money
raised would be donated to charity. I imagine that Kate is paying for
the venue and all other costs incurred by Homeground.

it would be fine to pay at the door. I'm sure they could use the advance
though, so send bucks if you can. No matter what...

you're a Love-Hound. GIVE YOUR NAME TO ME via e-mail or post.

A few days before the con I'll give Peter the master list of names and

venue will hold 2000. Andy's hopes of a small intimate atmosphere
seem to be out the window. I have my own private theory that Kate's
going to take a few years off to have kids and this is somewhat of

nearly certain that she'll hardly show her face in public again for
years. Anyone who can go, should.

If YOU have a passport and the money stashed away, you should treat 
yourself to a special present and go. Of course it's a "waste of money"
but when it comes to being in the same room as Kate Bush, who cares?
Chris and I "wasted" money when we drove to New York in 1985 to meet
Kate. We brought back a lifetime of Katefan memories though and haven't
regretted it for a single moment. 

Now for the Box Set...

It will be called "This Woman's Work" and will be released in early
November. The logo will be some sort of combination of the KT symbol
and the female symbol. Don't ask me...that's just what Peter said.
It should be priced at around 70 British pounds so we should all start
saving our pennies. It's almost certain that CBS in America aren't
going to have anything to do with the thing so we'll all have to pay
import prices (I'd buy import even if CBS did release it. CBS-phew!)
and there probably will be a limited number coming over to America.

Bad sad news--the track listing that Homeground printed IS in fact
the official list. Poor Andy & Derek, all that work for nothing!
The Set is supposed to have a very nice booklet with lots & lots of
photos and most likely a biography. Peter hasn't actually seen a copy
of the Set so he couldn't give any firm information about graphics etc.
That's all the info I have about that.

One more item that came to light in my talk with Peter. He & Krys knew
the second they looked at the "Tipper list" that it was a joke. They were
rolling on the floor laughing at the obvious in-jokes. They also got
Chris' "confession" so they knew he wrote it. They printed the list by
itself, without comment, just to see how people would react. It seems
that quite a few people took it seriously and wrote in very worried.
Peter assures me that he will reveal the truth about the list in the
next Homeground. I was certain that Peter must have gotten the joke
and it's nice to know that he did.
