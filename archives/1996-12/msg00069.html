I just read an article in InfoWorld dated March 4, 1996 by Brian Livingston.  
The gist of the article applies to all using Netscape Navigator and other 
browsers with the built-in functionality.

When a web page with a certain command (if you want exact syntax, E-mail me) is 
loaded, the browser automatically sends an E-mail to the person who created the 
WebPage IF the browser's E-mail functionality is active and accurate.  In other 
words, if you use Netscape to send or receive E-mail and you load one of these 
Web pages, you are automatically sending an E-mail to the vendor with your 
E-mail address.

To avoid distributing your E-mail address to potentially less than scrupulous 
vendors, remove the E-mail functionality from Netscape and use a different 
E-mail program, such as Eudora or Pegasus.  The other alternative is to use a 
browser without the E-mail functionality such as MS Internet Explorer 2.0 which 
doesn't have E-mail functionality yet.  Either that or enter a bogus E-mail 
address into Netscape and use a correct one in a signature file.  People can't 
automatically respond to your E-mail but, if they read, they could get a 
message to you.

If anybody is frequenting questionable content sites or doing it at work
without permission, you might want to be, um, discreet about it.  'Ware the
white wolf...
