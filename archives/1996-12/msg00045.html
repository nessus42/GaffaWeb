**I discovered Kate's music in the most accidental way--
I was shopping at a used-tape store and reached into a bin to pick up a
Bauhaus cassette for my sister.  I paid, left the store and was driving home
when I discovered I'd grabbed the wrong cassette and rather than Bauhaus, was
holding something called The Dreaming by a Kate Bush.  Rather than make a
fuss over exchanging a $3 cassette, I placed the tape in the player.  I
figured if the music was half as beautiful as the lady on the cover, it woul
be worth the three dollars.  I cannot even begin to express the sensation
that ran through my body and soul upon listening to Kate for the first time
(the tape was wound to Houdini.)  I have yet to determine how someone's music
can find its way into a recess of my heart (and still does upon each listen)
that I did not even know existed at the time.
It's been almost a decade now that I've been nourished by Kate's music.  It
never ceases to overwhelm me by its ever-lasting freshness and renewing
energy.  I can think I know her music inside and out, and then suddenly, one
day, I'll listen to a song I've heard 1000's of times and hear a lilt in her
voice, a warble, a hiss, an intonation on a certain syllable, etc., that I
had never noticed before.  I can spend days in reflection over one of her
phenomenal screams (IMHO--the best "scream" is in Constellation of the Heart,
followed by the one in Ken.)
And I haven't even heard the demos yet.

Does anyone know anything yet about plans for this years' Kate-mas parties?
 How can I get details?  Does anyone know if there's one planned in the upper
Midwest (U.S.A.) area?  

Thanks much!
--Samantha
