Then sjianuzi@mailbox.syr.edu (Nickie Curtis) asked: 
 
>Smurf, playing his guitar refrain...

>I've been listening to this line for over a year now...
>am I missing something?
>Why is it in such an emotional peice...
>Maybe someone can give an in  depth explanation of 
>the song "Moments of Pleasure"
 
       It's not Smurf, but 'there's Murph', referring to guitarist Alan
Murphy who died not long before the album was released.. "Moments Of
Pleasure" tells of the moments of our lives that we should treasure..
Memories of people who are longer be with us, but their memories will live
forever.. During the recording of TRS, Kate's mum also passed away, and she
is mentioned in the song as well. As is Bill Duffield ('Hey Bill, turn the
lights off'). He was a lightning-man who had a fatal accident during Kate's
Tour.
