 Some Zappa person writes:

 > The song was been recorded by Kate Bush (B-side of "The Hounds Of Love,"
 > 1985), who not only claimed composer  credit, but also left out the
crucial
 > second verse (which lets you know that the captain's wife, as well as the
 > captain, was hot for the cabin boy), the less essential final verse.

The false crediting of Kate Bush was of course an error by EMI.  Kate Bush
has
always been completely forthcoming about the sources for her music.  This
is borne out in the case of "The Handsome Cabin Boy" by the fact that, like 
Zappa, Kate herself played Ewan MacColl's version of this song during 
her guest-d.j. stint on Paul Gambaccini's BBC Radio One programme in 
December 1980.  (The version IED is referring to is the one sung by 
A. L. "Burt" Lloyd.)  And if that doesn't restore her unfairly 
tarnished reputation chez the Zappa group,  perhaps the news that
during her guest-d.j. appearance she extolled Zappa's talents and played his
"Montana" will.  Kate Bush -- generous to a fault, as always...
