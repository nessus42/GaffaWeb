On Tue, 4 Jul 1995, Anthony Kyle Monday wrote:

> Just a quick question: about six months ago, this was a moderated
> newsgroup, wasn't it? What happened?
> 

And "Stuart M. Castergine" <scasterg@cd.columbus.oh.us> answers:

>It's never really been a moderated group. The moderation flag is just 
>used to bounce the posts to a program that copys all the posts to the 
>mailing list before posting them to the newsgroup. It's completely 
>automated and none of the messages are filtered for content, length, etc.
>
>Given the increasing number of commercial posts on the group, active 
>moderation almost seems like a good idea. Annoying as these posts are to 
>the newsgroup readers, the ones who really suffer are the ones who get 
>teh mailing list, especially those who get it in digest format -- no way 
>to "just hit N".

I have a sollution that some might think is a bad idea:

We all agree (even all lurkers) to send a couple of copies each back to 
those post ads. 
Nuke them with their own posts!  ;-)
That way they will (hopefully) learn to stay away.

The only problem is: where to draw the line between an OK post and
those to nuke.

If we only had a mailing-list, it would have been relatively easy to 
install software to give writing/posting- permission only to those in
the mailing-list file ("members only"). But that would be in opposition
to the spirit of Gaffa.

Any other suggestions?
