Aficionados of the finer points of British culture will be pleased
to know that some KT music was heard on "The Archers" on Radio 4 last
week. Newly-wed Ruth Archer had the Bush music playing in the
background as she mused about the problems of living with in-laws and
began an argument with husband David. nd what was playing - why "Running
Up That Hill" or as it is commonly known RUTH. So Ruth listening to RUTH.
Is that significant, or IS that significant. Obviously a dedicated Love-hound
in the Archers' cast or writers. What more can we expect? Jack Wooley 
hosting Kate Bush conventions at Gray Gables, Eddie Grundy doing Kate
cover versions at the Cat and Fiddle, Elizabeth Archer doing a front page
spread on the new album in the Borchester Echo? I can't wait!

(For Americans who don't know what the hell I am talking about - well it's
a long story - 10,000 episodes to be precise).

The album title: who cares what it is called. I want to hear the music
not read the bloody cover. Mind you, I still think it should have been
called "Number 6".

Be seeing you.
Neil
