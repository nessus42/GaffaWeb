Robert Kudla asked:
> Just curious: Why in the world are people so concerned whether KaTe
> has written some gay-male-positive lyrics when she herself is female?
> It's not just the heterosexual males who would Just Die if she got
> married, either; I've seen some women posting in this thread.

  I haven't gotten the impression of "concern", just the usual sort
of traditional Love-Hound nit-piggling over the sort of nit-piggling
detail that Love-Hounds have always piggled nits over. 

> Kate is an artist. Kate probably knows *lots* and *lots* of gay men
> and lesbians, and maybe some of them are close friends with her.

  I don't think it would be jumping to conclusions to guess that her
dancer Gary Hurst, who died of AIDS, was gay. 

> Kate has written about Houdini, murder, incest, cloudbusting, dreams,
> (heterosexual) sexual relations, pregnancy, women struggling over
> various obstacles, surreal experiences, and a jillion other topics.  I
> think it's safe to assume she hasn't personally experienced some of
> these things, but has experienced others.  Why assume that writing
> about gay men means Something's Wrong?

 Kate is an artist with a great sensitivity to others, and has portrayed
almost all of the situations mentioned in a sympathetic or empathetic
manner. Kate doesn't just tell a story, she assumes a character and tries
to understand it, to see things from his/her perspective. Even the murderers,
criminals, bigots and lunatics in Kate's songs are attempts to understand
those feelings that exist in even the best of us.
