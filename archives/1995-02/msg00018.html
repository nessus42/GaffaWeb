Thread the Second:

Folks are wondering what's being flown in "Night of the Swallow".
Answer:  It doesn't matter.  It's what Hitchcock called "A McGuffin",
sort of a red herring which is central to "the plot", but really
just a device.  An example would be the briefcase in "Pulp Fiction"
or the car trunk in "Repo Man" - it doesn't matter what's in it, it
just matters that folks want it and/or are curious about it and/or
shouldn't have it.
