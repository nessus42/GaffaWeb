Hello KaTeFans...

what follows is a nice letter to Kate and you all from the drunken mob
at Katemas in Wollongong, Australia this year. We used a tag-team typing
system - sort of like tag team wrestling, except a paragraph instead of
a bif at a time.

Although KaTemas was the 30th, we waited to the next Saturday, the 1st,
to party. I had flown in from a Family funeral in New Zealand the night
before. This was the rather interesting result...

--------------------------------------------------------------------

G'Day there Gaffa, this is Steve from Gilmore St. Wollongong (near 
Syndey) Australia. Here it is, 10:44 PM on the Saturday after Kate's 
Birthday - our officially dedicated KaTemas party. I've just spent the 
last three days in New Zealand for a bloody powerful funereal 
experience and KaTemas somehow seems appropriate.

Currently there are a bunch of drunken KaTe fans in my lounge 
trying to annoy my next-door neighbours with a rather uncertain 
version of "Wuthering Heights". I've retold my story of me acting, 
singing and dancing as KaTe on stage last year doing WH, but 
pretending to be George Bush singing about his rather lusty 
relationship with Saddam Hussien. The details are generally 
obscense, but the chorus was somewhat like "Stormin' Normy its me, 
Bushy, come home now, let me scud your hoooole...". Anyway, this 
rather KaTe (and Beer) drunk host will now pass you over to your 
friend, and mine, Gra (Graham Dombkins).

Hi all! Well yet another KaTemas party here in "The Gong" has 
arrived in a sudden rush and with much panicked fanfare. It was 
only two days ago I was wondering around Sydney on KT's actual 
birthday and saying happy KaTemas to anybody that would listen. 
Next thing I know it is the night before KaTemas and all through my 
house I was hunting for things to go on the KaTemas-tree, we fixed it 
up quite well I believe with lots of tinsel, KT symbols and TWW 
symbols.

At the moment I'm listening to them in the next room murder yet 
another KaTe song with a drunken chorus of L'Amour Looks 
Something Like You. They seem to be able to hit every note -but- the 
right one! We have all been VERY well feed and now those that are 
still left, about 8 of us, are sitting in front of the fire in the lounge 
trying to out-sing each other in what-ever KaTe songs is playing at 
the moment.

This year we had about 18 people turn up for KaTemas, the one 
notable exception being the absence of John Quintal (where were you 
John?). The Music has ranged from her KaTeness to Tori Amos to 
KaTe to Peter Gabriel to KaTe to Mouth Music, .... We've had a tree a 
cake (with sparklers, thanks Steve) a BBQ and -heaps- of food and 
drink. I've been having a great time :-) ... Oh God!! Now somebody is 
trying to play BONGOS to the Kick Inside!! I think I'd better get back 
to the party before it goes totally out of control. Back to you Steve...

Steve back. Someone just started some Clannad - "Past-Present" - 
wonderful. I've learnt in the last 15 minutes that penny-whistle does 
not go easily with KaTe - that piano music of her drifts around too 
much for such a limited-key instrument. Never mind, I'll stick to 
learning some of her more "Irish" tunes. Anyway, back to the lounge 
room. There's about 7 people left next to the fire in the fireplace - 
the room is lovely and cozy, the only light coming from the fire and 
some candles, and the warmth from the friendship and the music. 
Tag!
