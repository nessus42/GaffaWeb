Keith wrote:

>I miss her.

So do I...She was my hero when I was in high-school, with her thick snakey
pigtails and her Blow-Yma-Sumac-Out-of-the-Water vocals.  She was by far
the best saxophonist on the Stiff label, any maybe even among all the New
Wavers back then.  That was a truly awesome time that marks my first
intense interest in Pop Music, thanks to monster records like More Songs
about Buildings and Food, the B-52's debut album, Elvis Costello's first
three, Look Sharp!  They paved the way for me to eventually find Brian Eno,
Laurie Anderson, Terry Riley, etc.  Stateless sure made me a happy
teen...until then I thought that ABBA, Foreigner and Rush were all there
was. Alas the only trace I have of Lene are two ticket stubs from 1988 when
she played the Ritz in New York, and two super compilations of her music
that Rhino released on CD in 1991.  Anybody know where she is?
