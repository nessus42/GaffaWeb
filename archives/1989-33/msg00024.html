Just received my copies of _TWW_ single on CD and picture disc. Imported
all the way from Austin, Dallas stores are pathetically sluggish in matters
Bushological. Interestingly, the CD lists "This Woman's Work (Single Mix)" 
and says "Original version of 'This Woman's Work' available on the album 'The 
Sensual World'" while the picture disc lists "This Woman's Work" and says
"'This Woman's Work' taken from the album 'The Sensual World'". What about 
the cassingle and the regular vinyl ? The CD single does sound somewhat 
different from the album and the picture disc does sound like the album, but
perhaps this is because I am telling myself it is so. My musically untrained
ear often cannot really distinguish fine variations in music.

_I'm Still Waiting_ receives a big WOOF of appreciation from this Love-Hound.

Perusing the surface of the picture disc for the "KT" symbol (I know there
isn't much chance of finding it on a single, but one never gives up hope, does 
one), I noticed some strange inscriptions on the vinyl surface, presumably
placed there as marks of authenticity. The strangest is the letter string
"UP YOURS UGLY" running from the fingers of her left hand over onto her left
eye.

I saw an ad for _TSW_ on VH-1 today. An ad by Target store, of all places !

They showed some shots from the Love and Anger video followed by a picture of

Edward_Lee_Whiteside@cup.portal.COM writes:

> Re: MTV cutting off the ends (and beginnings) of videos.  It is usually a

Sometimes MTV cuts off the "yeah" and sometimes they don't. The version I
taped does have the "yeah", with Kate turning to face into the camera and 
throwing a handful of the golden stuff right at the viewer. There is a very
interesting fade-out effect at that point, the golden stuff kind of pulling the
blankness (of the screen) with it. Reminds me vaguely of the effect on _The
Dreaming_ video when she says "Pull of the Bush" and the sunlight (?) seems to
be pulled into her face. If the laugh was on the video it would have to go
with a blank screen.
