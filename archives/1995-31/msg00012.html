P.S.:  Stay away from "The Secret World of Kate Bush and the Strange Art 
of Pop," unless you're interested in her genealogy.  Aside from the 
family background, the author spends a lot of time talking about her 
"sexual being" and presence, and not really a whole lot of her music.  
And with descriptions that's bound to turn off any feminist or any true 
fan, really, from the book.  (Kate herself has denounced this biography). 
 He only talks about "The Dreaming," and even his interpretations are 
boring.  (To hear what she has to say about "The Dreaming" and "Hounds Of 
Love" look for her picture interview CD in stores.)  N
