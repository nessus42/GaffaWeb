> Is it true that the best demo is Scares me silly (really gets me going).

I wouldn't say the best, but in some way it's outstanding. It belongs
to a group of six demos recorded with a full band. The other five songs
(Moving, Don't Push..., Kite, L'Amour... and Strange Phenomena) all 
showed up in their final studio version on The Kick Inside.
Maybe she planned (at least at that time) to release Scares Me Silly 
on her first album too. Maybe there's even a final studio version of it?
It's also unusually catchy, and the lyrics are directly based on
personal experience: a song about recording a song.
