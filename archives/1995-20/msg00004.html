This posting is a release of exasperation, nothing more.

While thumbing through the May 1995 VOGUE, I noticed an article about 
critics' darlings.  The article covered many celebrities from Aimee Mann 
to Jane Siberry to Woodie Allen.  The author defined a darling as one who 
is not appreciated by the general public but is lauded by critics. Odd, 
not a single mention of Kate Bush.  Doesn't she rate a single mention?  
Isn't she the critics' darling par excellence?  Regardless of ones 
appreciation of THE RED SHOES, wasn't the work, critically, very 
well-received?  

WHAT IS WRONG?  WHAT IS WRONG?  WON'T SOMEONE PLEASE TELL ME WHAT IS 
HAPPENING?  ARGHHH!

Thank you for reading my expression.

Keith
