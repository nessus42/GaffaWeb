Perhaps I need to look at it again, and I admit that I'm not the biggest
Hitchcock afficionado, but I stand by my opinion. It does have a rather
Bunuel-styled feel to the proceedings, but it's just never done anything
for me.

And while Kate is still no great shakes as a director (what she really
needs to do is direct somebody else's project--something she's not the
originator of), she's certainly gotten better. I like whole sections of
The Line..., and her video of "This Woman's Work" was a rather neat idea!

On the whole, most musicians who direct their own work have real problems
in making interesting pictures--witness the directorial attempts of Paul
McCartney, Frank Zappa, John Lennon (His "Imagine" film from 1971 makes me
wince, and Magical Mystery Tour is the KING of awful band-directed
films--), and countless others. 

Sometimes you just gotta admit that you're good at some stuff and not at
others, you know?
