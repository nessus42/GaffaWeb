Kate's video compilation "The Whole Story" was reviewed in the
Los Angeles Times on Tuesday, July 21 by Terry Atkinson:

Like this uniquely talented Englishwoman's music, her videos
delight and astonish...but they also disappoint and puzzle.
Bush's potential as a visual artist -- she's studied dance, mime
and film -- seldom seems fulfilled in these 13 videos, which only
sporadically reflect the scope of imagination in her songs about
dreams and the fantasy/reality contradictions of romance. On the
other hand, every one of these "failures" possesses the magic
missing from almost all other pop promos. Bush's personal vision,
humor and charming weirdness bring most of these mini-films alive,
if not kicking. However, this is _not_ "the whole story": Where's
the "Suspended in Gaffa" clip? And why just run one video after
another when Bush could have linked them with some special material,
if only a few words on her work? Information: (800) 847-4164. Rating:
v/ v/ v/ {three checks out of four possible}. -- Terry Atkinson.
