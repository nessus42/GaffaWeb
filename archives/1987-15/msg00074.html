Henry Threadgill by Gene Santoro

"... in the music Threadgill writes for and performs with his trio, Air,
his Sextett, his WindString Ensemble, his orchestra and his dance band,
as well as the film and radio and dance projects he's scored, you'll
catch echoes of the strangest things caroming off one another.  Whether
in his dense, swinging compositions or in his virtuosic excursions on
reed and flute, these meetings - between Stravinsky and King Curtis,
B-movie soundtracks and Muddy Waters, Bach and Ornette Coleman - help
create a unique and powerful voice. ..."

My typing falters but you should check out the rest of the article 
about the wonderful saxophonist/flautist/composer Henry Threadgill
and some of the music listed below.  I haven't heard all of them and
I've liked everything that I've heard so far but I think I like the
density of the music from the Sextett best.
