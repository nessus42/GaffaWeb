That's simple: The Whole Story was a "commercially correct"
product, consisting of single releases, not of so-called "album
tracks".
 
> I really don't think this album represents Kate's absolute best,
 
Neither do I, but from the singles that were released it was not so
bad a selection...
 
> NEW THREAD! NEW THREAD!
> If you had to put together a compilation album, with 15 tracks
> maximum, what songs would you put on it and in what order? And

It depends if it has to be sold. An artisticly correct album often
doesn't sell. So, I've come up with a solution for that: a double
album for the price of one. The first disc of 15 songs would then
include:
 
Running up that hill / Babooshka / The man with the child in his
eyes (the 7" version, at long last, on CD!) / Hounds of Love /
The sensual world / Wuthering heights / Wow // Them heavy people
("live") / Sat in your lap / Army dreamers (also the 7" version) /
Breathing / Cloudbusting / Rubberband girl / Rocket man / ...and a
new track, recorded for this album [Sorry Kate, but you have to
work again...] to promote the project. Hopefully a strong track
which'll become a hit.
 
The second, more interesting CD:
 
Be kind to my mistakes (film version; Never heard that one :-( ) /
The wedding list / Symphony in blue / Blow away / The kick inside
/ Waking the witch / Song of Solomon / Jig of life / Reaching out
/ Suspended in Gaffa / This woman's work / Warm and soothing /
Feel it / You're the one / Never be mine / All the love / One last
look around the house before we go... /
 
Well, it contains 17 songs, but the last one is so short... - But
_SO_ beautiful.
 
BTW, on the CD-single they could put "The seer" with Big Country (a
_brilliant_ song) and the "video" version of Cloudbusting... Ahhh,
just dreaming now...
 
I would have called it "Moments of pleasure" but left that track
out. So a nice alternative would be the name of the new track...
Unless that one's called "Experiment 5" ;-)
