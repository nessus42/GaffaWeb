Hello all.  I ran across an interesting Kate item today, and
knowing how you guys love Kate, I took a chance and bought this
item.  Here's what it is:

Keyboard magazine, July 1985
features 8 page article with Kate, several photos (I've never seen
her this young!).  Mint condition, it even still has the subscription
cards and a little vinyl 'record' in the center (remember those?).
no, it's not a Kate song, it's a jazz track.

Here's the article description:
"She's one of the best-kept secrets in contemporary rock, a flamboyant 
performer who hasn't appeared onstage in seven years, whose fans savor 
memories of spectacular concerts and complex conceptual albums.
Well, the long wait is over. Kate Bush's new LP is about to hit the
stores, and it's crackling with her own electronic and acoustic 
keyboard work.  In this special interview, Kate discusses her 
transformation from an Elton John-style pianist to a Fairlight 
enthusiast, and the impact of keyboard technology on her
artistic direction."

Though I enjoy her music, I am not a Kate-worshipper, so if anyone
is interested in purchasing this (I think rare) Kate item, email
me with your bids.
