The Led Zeppelin set (released about 2 weeks ago) is four disks with much of
LZ's music from their 10 albums and a few songs which were never released.
None of the songs were re-mixed, but all of them were re-mastered (by Jimmy
Page)- most from the original master tapes.  I notice better music fidelity
with this set as compared to earlier Zeppelin disks, but I have heard of others
who have bought the set and don't notice too much difference.  I have a fairly
high-end music system at home, and for me on my system the new set is far
superior sounding.  I paid $64 for the set, but I've since seen it other places
for less.  The cheapest price I've heard so far is the "price club" where it
was reportedly selling for $39.95.  The set comes in a large, album-sized box
with an album sized book of zeppelin interviews and pictures.  As for the disks
themselves:

I'm interested in hearing how many other KaTe fans out there also like the
Zep..  I find that for myself I listen primarily to KaTe and Zep music.
Even though I have a very large music collection, those are the performers
I keep coming back to.  Get this set if you like the Zep.
