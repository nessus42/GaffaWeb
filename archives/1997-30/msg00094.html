I keep saying it, but again--I don't think you can do any better, for
that sort of thing, than the two "extra" discs that come with the 8-disc
set "This Woman's Work". Put these together with the seven "real"
albums, and you have pretty much all the commercially released stuff. (I
can count the exceptions on one hand). This includes all the singles
(well over a dozen), the *On Stage* mini-LP, and the *Kate Bush* mini-LP
(with that outrageous outfit on the cover). Of the two two discs, the
longer of the two starts with "The Empty Bullring" and ends with "Be
Kind to My Mistakes". To me, it's between this "album" and *Hounds Of
Love* for which is her best "album". Hard to say these days which I like
better.
