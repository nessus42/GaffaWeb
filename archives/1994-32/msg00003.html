Faves:

>1.  This Woman's Work (gorgeous and fascinating "one-shot" illusion)
>2.  Running Up That Hill (simple and atmospheric, w/ excellent
choreography)
>3.  Experiment IV (her finest mini-movie, especially for a big
          horror-film buff like me...)
>4.  Babooshka (yum... anyhow, I had to have at least _one_ old one!)
>5.  Sat In Your Lap (absolutely mad...)

This is all well and fine, but below are the absolute best videos Kate
ever made:

1): There goes a Tenner. Great photography and a very claustrophobic feel 
     at all the right moments.
2): The Alternate Babooshka, where half her face is painted in lightning  
     bolts. (From a British Dr. Hook TV show. Right after that, the entire

     band interviews Kate. Ray Sawyer says, "We're just gonna ask you what
   
     other people always ask us. So, how long have you been together?"
3): Cloudbusting. I've seen it a thousand times and I could see it another

     time or two.
4): Hammer Horror. That male dancer in the black suit is the luckiest man 
     on the planet.
5): From "The Best of the Kenny Everett Show" Tape: A five second 
     appearance by Kate, where she says, "Hello, you remember me. I used 
     to be Kate Bush," in an overdubbed basso profundo voice.

HONORABLE MENTION:
Don't Give Up #1. Peter Gabriel is the second luckiest man on the planet.
What man (or woman, for that matter) would not jump at the chance of
holding Kate Bush to his body for over 6 minutes??? Gives me the willies
just thinking about it.
