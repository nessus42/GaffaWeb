I think that's true, but I think the song expresses a different
sentiment than you expressed.  IMO it's more like...

Don't want your mystique, your showing-off, your macho bullshit.  Give
me you, open up.  Do it just for me, because you want to do it *for*
me, and I can do it for you, just the same.

On a somewhat related note, the line
	"For I am sick of love"
that Kate quotes is translated as
	"For I am sick with love"
in my Bible, and in context clearly means love-sick and full of
passion.  I think Kate means the same by quoting it.  As one is sick
of the flu, love is the cause.  Of course she could have meant it as
being fed-up with love.  In US English it could be either.  You
decide.  Any UKers see it differently?
