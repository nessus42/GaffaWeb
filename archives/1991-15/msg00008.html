This Woman's Work II

From: John M. Relph <relph@presto.ig.com>
Date: Thu, 9 May 91 0:48:36 PDT
Subject: This Woman's Work II

Ok, folks, I've nearly completed work on the CD booklet for _This
Woman's Work II_.  This booklet has turned out to be much more
difficult than the first.  (But is that my fault?)

If you would like a copy of the PostScript source for the booklet,
please send me an e-mail message (no posting to Love-Hounds/
rec.music.gaffa please) and I'll send it to you as soon as it is ready
for beta-testing and final corrections.

So far there have only been very minor changes to the CD booklet for
Volume I, but if you'd like an upgrade, let me know.

In any case, I was listening to Volume II tonight, making final lyric
corrections, listening to "James And The Cold Gun" in the headphones,
and I was struck anew by the power of the music.  "Play it loud!"  No
shit.  Kate, how could you do this?  Live, no less.

And I also gave another listen to "Experiment IV".  How's this for a
new interpretation of the secret message: "I think the outline was
they're making an [unphonetic] instrument."  I still can't get a grip
on that penultimate word...

	-- John

"I'll bet my mum's gonna give me a little toy instrument!"
