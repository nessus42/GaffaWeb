Thus spake cbullard@HiWAAY.net (Len Bullard), adding some fire to the
Tori/Kate-debate..
 
[Tori Of Course]
>Yes.  She sang a completely forgettable song without
>range, tempo or pitch control in a dull dishwater alto.  The dress
>was ghastly and the hair was slept on.  Her posture was
>toad like and her ankles are fat like her face.  Does she ever smile?

     Soooo.. since when does the shape/size of someone's face or ankles
determine their artistic abilities, hmmmmm? Besides, these are the 90's.. A
woman can look however she wants to look.. Ghastly dress, wild hair..
Remember the German TV-performance where Kate dressed up as this
Mrs.Mopp-persona? Hardly glamorous, was it? And as for Tori smiling..
'Putting The Damage On' is a sad, bitter, song.. What's there to smile about?

>How did the line in Amadeus go, "... the Patron Saint of Mediocrity".

     She's the self-proclaimed Queen Of The Nerds actually.. But I guess you
missed that thread on Rmta.. . 

 >Why DO people compare Tori to Kate?  Well, there is the
>hair-thing.....
 
     The hair-thing? Both Tori and Kate are female singer/songwriters with
wonderful voices and heartwrenching songs, who choose to border on the
unconventional..

I can only agree with Norman (nbuchwa1@ix.netcom.com) when he wrote: 
>A different female artist with a similar voice is what appeals

>discussions.  She's more distinguishly different from Kate than Happy

      I've never known any other artist to be given so much badmouthing on

LoveHounds not HateMongrels..
