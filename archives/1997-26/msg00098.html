> Well, what a treat to be listed in the same title with my idol, Jane!
> 
> Thanks for your comments, Neal - glad your son could make it to the 
> show - chances are I talked to him at some point in the evening.  I had 
> a wonderful time playing at Seven Seeds - very appreciative audience, 
> great staff.  I'll be coming back there late October/November to promote 
> the new CD.
> 
> And what synergy... I GOT TO SEE JANE IN ATLANTA!  For an early birthday 
> present, my boyfriend had me skip work.  He wouldn't tell me why.  We 
> got on I-85 South (from Chapel Hill NC).  When we got to Charlotte (home 
> of my brother) he got on the exit ramp, then back on the highway.  OK, 
> we're not going to Charlotte I guess.  Then a few hours later, he gets 
> on the exit ramp for my parents in Greenville, SC, and back on the 
> highway.  As anyone who lives around there knows, the only logical next 
> guess was Atlanta.  Still don't know *why* until we pulled up at the 
> Village Playhouse in little five points.  The marquee read "Leo Kottke". 
>  As much respect as I have for Mr. Kottke, let's just say it was not the 
> result I was expecting from a 7 hour road trip (especially since he 
> plays our area fairly frequently).
> 
> However, as we drove by to park, I saw that on the *other* side of the 
> marquee was the sign for that night's show - JANE!!
> 
> She was incredible as always.  She had not performed in Atlanta in 9 
> years, and many people in the crowd had seen that last show (for The 
> Walking album.)  It was her, Tim Ray on piano, and Rebecca Campbell on 
> vocals.  She did mostly the newer stuff from Teenager and Maria, with a 
> few "throwbacks", namely Love is Everything from When I Was a Boy, and 
> You Don't Need from No Borders Here.  It would take several posts to 
> describe the concert - truly wonderful.
> 
> I got to talk to her after the show and got her to autograph her "Day in 
> the Life" CD I had just purchased. 
> 
> A wonderful early birthday present...
>
