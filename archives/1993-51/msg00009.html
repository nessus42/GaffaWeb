Not to be nit-picky and I do agree with Chris that there is no reason to
worry about copyright violations (how the heck could the person prove that
there were damages!) BUT....  there is no requirement in copyright law to
provide a "copyright notice."  They can be useful for evidence but do not
create any new legal rights.  As soon as one puts a work of writing in any
"fixed form" it is automatically copyrighted.  Here, though, only an absolute
fool would bother to sue since he/she couldn't prove any real "damage" and
wouldn't get any dough.  Besides suing over something like this would be 
silly.  So post away.
