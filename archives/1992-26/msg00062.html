Hey there all you Gaffans -
  I have in my possession five Kate Bush picture disks (you remember
LP's, don't you?), upon which is recorded about 20 minutes of inter-
view with KT.  I don't recognize the voice of the interviewer, but he's 
clearly a Brit.
  As for the pictures, on one side is a nice full-face shot of Kate,
wearing a red polka-dotted scarf, tied in a bow about her neck.  The
other is a figure shot of Kate in her slinky red dress with the big
acoustic bass.
  Why am I selling these, rather than telling you all where YOU could
have bought them (thus eliminating me, the middleman)?  Because my
source for these goodies is a wholesale dealer who only sells recorded
music in large blocks (>50 items).
  If anyone is interested, I'll let them go for $15 apiece with the
standard cardboard outer jacket, plus a plastic dust jacket, postage
included (to U.S.A. only--sorry to others but overseas shipping rates 
are too hard to predict).
  One more thing--the wholesale catalog listed them as "Limited
Edition," (along with lots of other relatively uninsteresting artists' 
stuff) but frankly I have been unable to find a serial number or the 
like that would indicate the truth value of that claim.
