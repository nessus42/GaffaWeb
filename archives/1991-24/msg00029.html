    Just one more piece of evidence to back up the above assertion:

    In the RUTH video, about a minute and a half into it, there is a 
definite crucixion motif.  What do I think backs up this interpretation?

	The position the man is in.  Plus he is facing away from the 
	camera, kind of depersonalizing (is that a word?) him into the
	object of a cross.

	The position Kate is in.

	The walls in the background seem to have some clouds painted
	on them. Also the lighting flickers, as with lightning, an
	outdoor phenomenon.  This seems to move Kate from the dance
	room to the (outdoor) Calgary hill.

	Further along in the sequence she looks towards the camera
	which is now above her.  This is analogous to Christ's crying
	out to God the Father while on the cross.

	After Kate is lowered the next scene is of the Savior after she
	has risen again.

    I guest this just proves what most of us have known all along. :^)
