that was me. i'm glad you liked the Black Tape stuff... their first
album is less connected (in my mind) and the sounds are more scattered.
in that, i mean, that they jump from things like acoustic guitar songs
to new agey stuff... this new one is much more tight in that respect.
but i like both. in any case, the only two albums by Black Tape that
i'm aware of (perhaps Vicki can correct me if i'm wrong) are:

ASHES IN THE BRITTLE AIR
A CHAOS OF DESIRE

they also have a track on a sampler called um, FROM ACROSS THIS GRAY
LAND NO.2. what i'm most impressed with in Projekt Records is that the
quality of the packaging (art and the booklets, etc.) is really well
put together as is the music. it makes it a worthwhile purchase. in
any case i realise this isn't nm-list but i have to plug a new Z'ev
CD i recently purchased from a live concert last year. it's called
OPUS 3 and it's just increadible. if you like percussive music or
even say, taiko or kodo drumming you will probably like this. Z'ev
is more experimental, but to me there are shades of kodo in it. this
album is a bit different from his usual fare, but it's an increadible
72 minutes of him pounding away on all kinds of instruments or made
/found objects. i believe it's on Staaltape.
