In article <1991Jan31.192129.17127@cbnews.att.com> you write:
>Really-From: nrc@cbema.att.com (Neal R Caldwell, Ii)

>Someone else mentioned a Japanese copy of _The Single File_ that they
>had purchased recently which sounded like a bootleg.  I purchased
>copy of it ($40) about six months ago mail order from "The Video Depot" 
>or some such now defunct place.  It appeared to American in origin but
>in spite of the wishful thinking of many Love-Hounds and a note in
>Homeground, I'm almost certain that it's a bootleg.

Well, let's be clear about this.  The British PAL version of _The
Single File_ and the Japanese NTSC version of this are *not* bootlegs.
They are real releases by EMI.  Heck, they're shown in the notes for
_The Whole Story_ album.  It's the recent American release of this
video that now appears to be a bootleg, although there was some hope
a while ago that this had finally been released legitamately in
America.

>If I recall correctly the videos on _TSF_ that are not on _The Whole
>Story_ are, _Rolling the Ball_,

This mistake seems very common nowadays, and I'm not sure why.  The
name of this song is "Them Heavy People".  I'm sure you know that,
Richard!  If this is the title of the song given on your copy of
_The Single File_, then you're right: it's probably a bootleg.  The
title is given correctly on the legitamate British and Japanese versions.
