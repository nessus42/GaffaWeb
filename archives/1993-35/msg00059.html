Fiona sez...
>I would also hope, without getting into the great debate of "what is
>an opinion, what is a valid opinion, what is a fact", that people could
>express misgivings about her work without it being suggested that
>love-hounds turn into alt.bash.kate. There is a difference between
>saying "Kate is useless and a talentless poseur" and saying "I really
>like and appreciate what she has done before but this new music doesn't
>do it for me".

    Yeah, I to am getting a little sick of what seemed to be becoming out right
KaTe bashing. I still haven't got my copy of ETM yet so I can comment greatly
on the debate except to say that what I've had of it (11Khz Mac 'snd ') hasn't
made me want to -hate- it yet. It's different. It gets in. (After the first
time that I heard it, it wouldn't get out of my head!!) :-)

    I can say this for RBG, it's VERY contagious as a song. I've played it here
at work and got it played to about 800 people at Uni Movies during the break
and many people whom normally wouldn't consider themselves as KaTe music buffs
have asked me who it was. They LIKED it! :-) At the same time BSL is there for
the long tie fans to latch onto. Sort of like a guiding path that  helps lead
the fan on the way to the new music.

    How lucky can you get!? :-) When I saw her in Sydney it was a wonderfully
intermit concert. Sort of like being at a Tori concert. I waited till most of
the people had left at the end of the show and went to see if I could meet her.
She was VERY kind and came and said hello, we had a bit of a chat and then she

couldn't resist asking if she'd do the others. She was amazed that somebody had
them all. :-)
