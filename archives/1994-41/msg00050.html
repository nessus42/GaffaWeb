    kln@crl.com recently asked about the reference to 'goose moon' in the
    lyrics of "L'Amour Looks Something Like You"...
    I looked through my copy of _Brewer's_Dictionary_of_Phrase_and_Fable,
    and although there's no direct reference to goose moon, there are
    some other 'goose' refs that indicate it has to do w/ a full moon
    around September 29, aka Michaelmas Day. Geese were generally fatted
    and plentiful 'round this time, which was a 3/4 year day when rents
    were due, accounts being settled, etc... this seems to fit (to an
    extent) with the lyrics.  Hope this helps, Karen.
