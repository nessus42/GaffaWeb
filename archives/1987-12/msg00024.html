>I realize your

No not that different really. Our National Council for Civil Liberties
takes exactly the same line as your ACLU. It is just that some of the
views that are expressed by extreme groups scare the shit out of me.

>Unfortunately, too many U.S. citizens (including 
>some elected office holders) don't really believe in freedom of speech when 
>it comes to things which upset them.  

Agreed. Likewise in most if not all countries that claim to be democracies.
My real fear is that freedom of speech is only a reality for the
privileged or the powerful. As long as everyone gets a fair opportunity
to defend themselves then there is no need for censorship.

>> ... and then seeing the NF's
>>papers and pamphlets being sold in their local markets. ...
>Yeah, this is where liberty gets hard to live with.  But then if you can't 
>handle the hard parts, you can have it much easier in a regimented society

Aargh! You were doing fine up till here! Chile ? Well things might be better
there if the CIA had not overthrown the Allende (sp?) government. South
Africa? Well life for the black population might not be so grim if 
governments like my own and yours took more positive steps to show people
like Botha that their policies are complete anathema to civilised societies.

(N.B. no personal flame or anti-Americanism intended - Mrs Thatcher welcoming
Botha to GB last year sickened me. Not for the fact that they were talking,
but for her refusal to openly condemn his regime.)

>Make that libertarian vs. liberal; it's almost impossible.  It hasn't happened

>damned best and hope for some success.  

Amen to that.
