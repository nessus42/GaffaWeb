Subject: When I first heard KaTe...
To: love-hounds@eddie.mit.edu

 
I was first exposed to KaTe when watch Night Flight.  They where showing 
her Experiment IV video.  I was amazed!  What a beautiful voice, and 
the concept of the video/song was very interesting.  I knew right 
away the this person wasn't just another "sex, love, pop, ad naseum" 
singer.  The problem was, I didn't catch her name!  I remembered the 
title of the video, but not the artist.  Sogoing to my local Sam not-
 
so Goody, I asked if there was a catalog of songs/albums by title 
alone.  I looked for Experiment IV, but nothing.  Oh well, I sighed, 
rationalized and said that she probably isn't that good anyway.  (Boy 
was I ever wrong ;->)  A couple of months later, just walking around 
in another store, I saw a CD cover with the picture of a beautiful 
woman.  I picked it up and BANG!  Track 10 was "Experiment IV"!  Oh 
joyeous day!  I quickly skipped to the cashier and popped down my 
$16.  All I could say the first time I listened to the CD was:  "Kate 

Bush, oh what a feeling!" and jumping wildly.  Needless to say, the 
people from Toyota got a hold of my comment and immediately sued me 
for trademark infringement.  ;;-))
 
p.s. - Has anyone noticed the secret comment about a well-known Irish 
band in All We Ever Look For?  She is quoted in saying: "All we ever 
look for -- a little bit of U2;..."  The real strange thing is that 
U2 responds to Kate: "But I still haven't found what I'm looking for..."
Hmmmmm...  Could it be they're both looking for a better Recorder/
 
Mixer for their next albums?  Hmmmm....
