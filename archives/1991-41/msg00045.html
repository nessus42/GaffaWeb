This is the latest version of the Renaissance US Discography which
I originally compiled in 1982.  I believe this to be up-to-date as
of the beginning of May, 1991.

Many (if not all) of the band's Sire recordings have just been
reissued on CD in Japan.  I am currently trying to verify titles and
catalog numbers, so there will be an update in the near future.

(If anyone in netland has the Japanese reissues,
your indulgence in this matter would be greatly appreciated :-).


Joe Lynn

1969	Keith Relf - vocals, guitars, harmonica
	Jim McCarty - drums, percussion, vocals
	John Hawken - piano, harpsichord
	Louis Cennamo - bass guitar
	Jane Relf - vocals, percussion

	released:	"Renaissance" (1969) Elektra EKS-74068
				produced by Paul Samwell-Smith
				CD reissue: Line LICD 9.00421 (W Germany-1987)
					Kings & Queens;  Innocence;
					Island;  Wanderer;  Bullet


1971	Keith Relf - vocals, guitars, harmonica
	Jim McCarty - drums, percussion, vocals
	John Hawken - piano, harpsichord
	Louis Cennamo - bass guitar
	Jane Relf - vocals, percussion
	*Don Shin - electric piano

	+(alternate lineup:)
	Neil Korner - bass guitar
	Michael Dunford - guitar
	T. Slade - drums
	T. Crowe - vocals
	
	+This lineup performed one track on the LP below ("Mr. Pine");
		the remaining tracks were by the original members.
		This recording also marks the first appearance of
		lyricist Betty Thatcher.

	released:	"Illusion" (1971) Island (UK) HELP 27
				produced by Paul Samwell-Smith
				CD reissue: Line LICD 9.00425 (W Germany-1987)
					Love Goes On;  Golden Thread;
					Love Is All;  Mr. Pine;  Face of
					Yesterday;  Past Orbits of Dust


	At this point, the original lineup departed.  McCarty, Relf,
		and Cennamo went on to form the band _Armageddon_.
		McCarty, however, continued contributing songs to
		the 'new' Renaissance until 1974.  After
		Keith Relf's death in 1976, the original
		Renaissance reformed as _Illusion_, whose work
		is discussed at the end of the discography.



1972	John Tout - keyboards, vocals
	Annie Haslam - lead vocals, percussion
	Rob Hendry - guitars, mandolin, chimes, vocals
	Jon Camp - bass, vocals
	Terence Sullivan - drums, percussion
	*Francis Monkman - VCS 3
	
	released:	"Prologue" (1972) Sovreign/Capitol ST-11116
				produced by Miles Copland and Renaissance,
				arranged by Michael Dunford
					Prologue;   Kiev;  Sounds of the Sea;
					Spare Some Love;  Bound For Infinity;
					Rajah Khan

 	Michael Dunford was briefly a member of the band until he
		decided to quit and concentrate on his songwriting;
		his replacement, Mick Parsons, was killed in an
		accident right before _Prologue_ was recorded.
		Rob Hendry was Parsons' last-minute replacement.


1973	John Tout - keyboards, vocals
	Annie Haslam - lead vocals, percussion
	Jon Camp - bass, vocals
	Terence Sullivan - drums, percussion
	*Michael Dunford - acoustic guitar
	*Andy Powell - guitar

	Despite his work with the band since Renaissance Mk. I, Michael
		Dunford was not a "regular" band member until 1974.

1981	Annie Haslam - lead vocals
	Jon Camp - bass & electric guitars, vocals
	Michael Dunford - acoustic and electric guitars, vocals
	Peter Gosling - keyboards, vocals
	Peter Barron - drums, percussion, vocals

	released:	"Camera Camera" (1981) IRS/A&M 70019
				produced by Renaissance
				CD reissue: Line LICD 9.00042 (W Germany-1988)

					the Bottom of the Garden);  Remember;

					Okichi-san;  Jigsaw;  Running Away

	Gosling and Barron were replaced by Mike Taylor and Gavin
		Harrison, respectively, for the band's 1982 US Tour.

1985-	Annie Haslam - lead vocals
 1987	Michael Dunford - acoustic and electric guitars, vocals
	Raphael Rudd - keyboards, harp
	Mark Lampariello - guitar, bass

	no recordings released

	This band lineup toured the UK and USA during 1985 and 1986;
		a couple of UK appearances took place at the Royal
		Festival Hall in London.  The final Renaissance
		performance was held at the Club Bene in Sayreville, NJ
		on June 6, 1987.
