So has anybody figured out the legal aspects (if any) of using the
record cover pictures on the T-shirt?  I hope it shouldn't matter 
since we're not trying to make money (are we? :-).  Or, perhaps we can
contact Kate Goddess Bush (wait, that's KGB!) herself and get her
permission.

W.r.t T-shirt designs, I like IED's first idea.  Comments:
1) on the back, I think the quote ("with a kiss ...") should be the
center of attention, and written in solid black ink.  The KT logo
would be sort of semi-existent (e.g. dotted in) so that to the
uninitiated, the logo appears only as a sort of an abstract design
quitely permeating the back.  It would not compete with the quote
for attention lest the design on the whole gets too crowded.
(what's da term they use ... "negative space").

2) On the front, "Love-Hounds" letters are as big as the letters in the
quote on the back.  "An Inter .... Forum" are smaller, perhaps all fit
on one line, or even moved below the picture.  The picture would, of course,
take up most of the space.  Something like:
