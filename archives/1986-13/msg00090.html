And thus did he (almost) speaketh the truth ...

In fact, considering the insidious `Import Ban' me & Sue T. have been
talking about, you probably couldn't get these 2 records from overseas
if you tried ...

Anyway.  Touch & Go, in association with Baby records (the Prunes' current
label, they license out to the record company - previously it was New Rose
in France), has recently released the new Virgin Prunes LP and 12" single:
	`Love Lasts Forever' Pts. 1 & 2  12"	Baby 004/Touch & Go T&G #15
	`The Moon Looked Down And Laughed'  LP	Baby 005/Touch & Go T&GLP #9

The verdict:
	Buy the 12".    Evokes fond memories (at times) of Prunes past.
	Avoid the LP.   The current VP lineup consists of Gavin Friday (voc),
			Strongman (bass), Mary (drums?) and `Pod' (gtr?).
			The first three are holdovers from the last great
			Prunes lineup, but unfortunately they have since lost
			Guggi (voc), Dik (gtr), and Dave-ID Busarus (narrator,
			voc), although Dave-ID makes a vocal appearance on
			1 track.  The biggest problem is loss of Dik, who was
			the lynchpin of the whole sound (if anyone ever hears
			`Heresie', you'll know what I mean).  The album as
			a result is very musically `thin'.
The Princess Tinymeat tie-in here is that Bintii (real name Haa Lacka Bintii)
was the percussionist for the Prunes in their early stages, before the
release of the `If I Die, I Die' LP.
