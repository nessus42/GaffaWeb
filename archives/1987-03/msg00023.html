Re. Peter Lee's XTC-on-BBC tape request: I'm also very interested.  If either
	the taper (tapir?) or Mr. Lee could send me a copy I'd be eternally
	grateful.

Re. someone's query about "Deutsch-Amerikanische Freundschaft": DAF were, as
	far as I know, a German post-Kraftwerk electronic-pop group.  I
	haven't heard any of their stuff, but I know that one of the two
	people in the group put out a solo album in '83 or '84 which contained
	a song called "Darling Don't Leave Me" on which Annie Lennox sang
	and which thus got airplay.  Good early-Eurythmics-like song.

Incidentally, as I posted to net.music, both the Smiths and U2 should have
new albums out next month.  The Smiths one will be a double album with both
new songs and hard-to-find b-sides and obscurities.  Ho hum -- I've lost
so much interest in the Smiths of late.  The U2 album should be good, though,
as Eno's producing again (hell, all U2 albums are good!).
