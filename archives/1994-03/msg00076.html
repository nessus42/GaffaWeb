Fionia writes about the second RBG video:
>
>If I were not a Kate-lover, but instead your typical couch potato
>MTV/Muchmusic viewer, I would be sucked in by the fast edits and the beat
>at the start of the video. But toward the end where the bits from The
>Line, The Cross, The Curve are edited in, I would be going "Huh? Who's
>this babe in the feather dress? Who's this old lady? Who's this guy in
>front of the curtains? What does this have to do with anything?"
>
>Sure, the record. co wants to promote the film, but it doesn't make much
>sense to plop bits of it into a completely unrelated context. I'm not sure
>that it serves the film, the song, or the artist much. 

   Actually, *Kate* wanted to promote the film, the record company
wanted to promote the album. Columbia exhibited zero interest in showing
the film now that Kate is back in the UK. I called the squid in charge
of arranging showings, and he told me that all showing were canceled,
and that they weren't even sure that they would even release it on tape.
   Columbia would probably prefer a film-free version of the video,
but were afraid to ask Kate for *yet another* version.
