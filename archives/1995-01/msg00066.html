In a previous posting,  (JHILEM41@MAINE.MAINE.EDU) writes:
>> I was wondering what people think of Milla's CD "The Divine Comedy"?
>> I realy enjoy this CD and would like to read what others think.

I've greatly enjoyed this album, it is definitely going to be one of my 
alltime favourites.  She's very promising in her musical direction, and 
as she get's older a wiser, I'm sure her lyrics will be very well thought 
and interesting.  It's an CD like no other, and definitely well worth 
trying if you like Kate.

In article <D24n4r.8w0@freenet.carleton.ca>,
David J. Zimmerly <am672@freenet.carleton.ca>

>I quite like it as well. It has a spunky daring to it that is a close
>enough reminder of The Kick Inside to bring a smile of to those of us who
>remember that first thrill in '78.  Of course, with 5000+ mutant strains
>of so-called "alternative" music now the norm, poor Milla has already
>found out that to sound as "different" as Kate did when Wuthering Heights
>pealed across radioland is a lot harder to do today.
>
>It will be interesting to hear that oh-so-crucial second album...
>
>David

When the CD was new, I read so many reviews on it, and they were all very 
positive.  It's too bad that the album didn't seem to make too big a 
splash.  I thought it would, especially in the University/College crowd.  

I'm a little worried that the desire for success might cause Milla to 
change her style a lot, but then again, to have done what she did in the 
first place must have taken a lot of guts and standing her ground.  
Perhaps she won't be easy to shake.

Here's hoping she hits the big-time.

Kelly.
