Tara Marchand posted a great article on Sarah McLachlan. THANKS! 
I have a couple of comments and questions:

>The stage dims to a sedate hush of shadow as a cloaked figure
>touches a long, thin match to one candle after another.
[...]
>When a slight, quiet-voiced woman steps out in an ankle-length
>black dress and worn black leather boots, carrying a guitar, you
>almost expect her to be joking.  But then she begins to sing.

I hate to say this but reading this alone reminds me of the Sinead tour...
Although it is unfair to Sarah to compare her with Sinead, comparisons 
are inevitable. 'tis the twists and jumps of the voice that make them 
sound so alike. And apparently they have similar tastes in stage antics.
Not to mention that the cover of 'Touch' reminds me of the outdoors scenes
from 'Nothing compares 2 u'... They are both so similar and so different! 
And both so incredibly talented.

>Watch the touring calendars for McLachlan's upcoming appearances,

AND  Sarah McLachlan in concert in 1992, is more than I could ask for!
