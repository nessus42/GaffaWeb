All ties were broken first by anyone who wanted to change their votes
or vote for more songs (indicated by an asterisk), then by number of
people who voted on the song, and then by a flip of a coin.  (All
tails in the air but when the penny landed it's still dancing . . .

Titles with slashes indicate different titles given for the same demo
recording.  Versions featured with a number of votes represent a
minority.

Commentary on the results:  It appears that eighteen years after the
fact,  "Wuthering Heights" is STILL the Lovehounds' favorite, her most
successful single to date.   "Cloudbusting" clearly won by a landside,
with Kate's retelling of Peter Reich's "A Book Of Dreams" and the
guest star of Donald Sutherland.  When it came down to minialbums,
"The Ninth Wave" beat "The Dreaming."  The winners of least liked
songs clearly had some disagreement with other Love Hounds since all
three of the tunes are featured on the top 100,  Kate's covers of both
Elton John tunes captured almost half of the voters, period.  And
while Kate has guested more often than we seem to remember, "Don't
Give Up" is our favorite, hands down.  Much to my surprise, no one
voted for her duet with Midge Ure ("Sister and Brother"), but that's
what happens when one choice is available, I guess.
