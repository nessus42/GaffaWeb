The Story's two albums with Jennifer Kimball as part of the duo are
wonderful (especially _Grace In Gravity_, whose title track brings me to
tears every time).  I just couldn't get into _Plumb_, maybe because I'm
biased since the last time I saw The Story was right before Jennifer left,
and I thought they should've left that damn band at home.  The harmonies
just aren't the same without Jennifer, even though Jonatha Brooke does have
an amazing voice.  (Note to Americans: There's a new set of Goodyear tire
commercials that's been getting a lot of airplay during sports events and
the like, and the silly jingle (something about "freedom", with a fanfare
of "Goodyear!" at the end) is sung by none other than Jonatha Brooke, who
must be more strapped for cash than I'd thought.  Just thought I'd drop
that little factoid into the mix. :)

Very few men get airplay in my life, but those that do include Roy Harper,
Richard Shindell (who Really Is if Kate ever drops off the face of the
earth), Robyn Hitchcock, David Sylvian, and Robert Fripp.  Hmmm... lots of
"r" names there.  Never noticed that before. :)
