Yep, go buy something of Happy's. The Borders in my town (Plano TX) has a 
whole bunch of Happy's stuff. That's the only place I've seen her. It's 
worth it even if you end up not liking her, just to hear her do some of 
the vocal things she does. Each register that she uses is very distinct. 
At times she sounds like four or more different people.

In addition to Vickie's analysis, I would suggest there's a lot of Jon 
Anderson (Yes) in Happy's past. She phrases like him at times. Cross Kate 
with Jon Anderson, David Bowie, and Annie Lenox and you get Happy.
