OK, While we're onto culture, the blues, teen angst, etc. etc., consider
this little philosphical conundrum:

Many have commented that it almost seems to take great sufffering to
make great art. The blues is the fount of so much creativity, for
instance; and after all, when did you ever hear of a really cookin' rock
band from, say, Switzerland? No way! On the other hand, not everybody
who does great stuff is all messed up. Is Kate the exception that proves
the rule, or do we need to revise the rule? Other than having her mother
die (at least, thankfully, not when Kate was very young--and after all,
this is not an unusual sorrow, we have to say), I don't know what has
ever happened to Kate that is really very bad at all. Her life almost
seems the picture of tranquility, by all accounts. And yet she somehow
did not turn out as some spoiled rich kid. Maybe money doesn't explain
everything after all.)
