- In article <1254@otc.otca.oz> wayner@otc.otca.oz (Wayne Robinson) and
- in article <920@appleoz.oz.au>, ksand@appleoz.oz.au (Kent Sandvik) cough up
- the party line that musical ability is unrelated to presentation - i.e. just
- because everyone, regardless of sex, would like to root kylie, that doesn't
- alter the fact that she sounds godawful.
- 
- What these folks have failed to understand is that music is about sensory
- stimulation. This simple fact is the sole justification for such emminently
- successful phenomena as light-shows, dance parties, MTV, opera, and ballet.
- Gender-free sex-symbols synergetically enhance ANY music. Kylie is only
- the latest example in an ages-old chorus of dancing pop-tarts, and I for one
- fail to see anything wrong with that. If "the Kylie phenomenon" is competition
- for "new music", then it is only Hobson's choice for anyone with the temerity
- to shell out bucks for entertainment.
- 
- Of course the synergy works the other way, too. If Kate Bush couldn't sing
- would she be anywhere near as attractive? I don't think so. Where's the harm?
