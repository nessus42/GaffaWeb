     IED thought newer Love-Hounds might enjoy reading a few examples
of Kate's responses to fans' questions, as they appeared originally in
Kate Bush Club Newsletters. Here are two or three, and IED will try to
print a bunch more soon.

-------------------------------------------

Q.: I have just finished reading Shakespeare's _Othello_. In the scene
just before Othello kills Desdamona he says, "Put out the light/Then
put out the light". I was wondering if this means the same thing in
"Blow Away"?

A.: You're the first person in four years to pick up on this -- so,
thank you.

Q.: What was your favourite record of 1983?

A.: "101 Damnations" by Scarlet Party.

Q.: Is the man featured on _The_Dreaming_ cover, in the Houdini Pose,
Del Palmer?

A.: That's for me to know and you to find out.

Q.: Why do you always move your eyes right and left in your videos? It
is very pleasant to watch, but it intrigues me. What is the idea
behind it?

A.: I have to watch out for any demons that might be creeping up on me
and video shoots attract so many of them that I have to keep an extra
eye out in case they trip me up while we're going for a take. You've
seen what happens to Faith Brown because she doesn't look out for
them.

Q.: Have you ever thought of releasing a live L.P.?

A.: Yes.

Q.: What is sung during "Delius", after the bit where Delius is
dictating to Fenby?

A.: Can't you tell?

Q.: What kind of make-up do you use?

A.: I don't bother any more, I use Latex!

Q.: Do the birthday and Christmas cards and presents you
are sent actually reach you?

A.: All the ones I've received have reached me.

Q.: Which is your favourite cover version of one of your songs?

A.: "Poor Old Flea" by Madame Maria Nanky.

-------------------------------------

     Love-Hounds can see why it's sometimes frustrating trying to get
a Bushological problem solved by going to the source for a straight
answer. Admittedly the above selection highlights Kate's most
uncommunicative side. Sometimes she actually gives a question a
substantive answer. But when those are interspersed with the above, it
seems unwise to rely on the "serious" replies, as well.

-- Andrew Marvick
