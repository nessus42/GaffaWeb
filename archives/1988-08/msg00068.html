     First, an erratum to acknowledge: IED reported a couple of weeks
ago the appearance on the market of another KT interview picture-disk.
He described the two photos as being "recent". In fact, only one is
recent (1985-6), the other is a familiar shot of Kate ca. 1978,
wearing her old dove earrings.
     Apropos of that record, the same 1986 interview has now appeared
on a 3" compact disk, in a 5" "economy" jewel-box (same size as but
thinner than a normal jewel-box) with adaptor and plain white-paper
cover inside that says simply "Kate Bush Interview 1986", printed (as
was the sticker on the cover of the picture-disk) in violet "Hounds of
Love"-style script. The product bears no label name, but says "Made in
UK" and has a catalogue number "KB3CD". The interview on the disk is
identical to the picture-disk's, but the inside of the cover-card in
the CD box has a short, illiterate and anonymously authored blurb
about Kate and the interview.
     This same 3" compact disk is also reported to have become
available in "gold", i.e. with a gold tint to the CD.  This makes a
total of _five_ different KT interview CDs, all of which have come out
in the past six or eight months.

-- Andrew Marvick
