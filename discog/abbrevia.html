Labels

There are five major different standard labels on the vinyl discs. They are named as following:

yellow label
yellow label with black printing, 'Kate Bush' is mostly in script style, but not always. It's mentioned if it's not script style. Around the edge there's usually a red printing about ownership and rights on the recording. These labels are usually 'Made in EEC' and occur since the Hounds of Love album (as well as on rereleases)
orange label
orange/brown label with big red EMI on one half and black printing on the other half of the label. This is the earlier European label for Kate discs from EMI, Japanese discs with this label are brown, Belgium discs with this label are intense orange.
black label
black label with silver printing. This kind of label can usually be found on British releases
grey label
grey label going from dark grey at the top to light grey at the bottom. There's a rainbow colored EMI America logo on the top and another silver-brown EMI America logo on the right. The printing is black. This can be found on most EMI America (USA & Canada) releases
silver label
silver labels with black printing. Usually found on French 7" vinyls. The usual sentence about copyright etc is in the vinyl directly around the label itself.

Other labels are described when they occur. "xxx/yyy labels" means that the label is of colour xxx and the printing on it is of colour yyy.

Abbreviations in the description field:

There are four different kind of inner sleeves for vinyls, abbreviated as following:

no inner
there's no inner sleeve, the discs reside directly in the cover
paper
a white paper inner sleeve
plastic
a plastic inner sleeve
p&p inner
a white paper inner sleeve laid out with plastic

Other inner sleeves are described when they occur.

7" vinyl singles

7" vinyl singles can have three kinds of central holes:

small hole
LP style hole of ~0.8 cm diameter
big hole
music box style hole of ~4cm diameter
break hole
small hole that can be made into a big hole by breaking out the inner part.

Japanese vinyl

Japanese vinyl discs often have a separate lyrics sheet with Japanese and English lyrics, another picture, maybe an article in Kanji and credits or so. This is abbreviated as J-E lyrics sheet

Slim double jewel packs

type 'G' is like a normal thick jewel pack with the insert opening to the right.

type 'E' the insert opens to the left,

type 'S' the insert opens on the back. (The uppercase letters do represent the way the plastic is wrapped.)

Numbering

This part will explain the codes used in the Discography to you.

Numbering scheme:

a.b.c.d.e, with:

a = category:
1 = album
2 = single/EP
3 = related work
4 = sampler
5 = bootleg

b = title:

Album
1.1 = The Kick Inside
1.2 = Lionheart
...
Single
2.1 = Wuthering Heights / Kite
2.2 = Wuthering Heights / The Man With The Child In His Eyes
...

Related work
3.1 = Lesley Duncan: Sing Children Sing / Rainbow Games
3.2 = Peter Gabriel: Games Without Frontiers / Lead a Normal Life
...

Samplers
4.1 = Poslushaj muzgku (flexidisc) [Russian blue flexi w/ Babooshka]
4.2 = 4 Successos Vol IV
...

Bootlegs
5.1 = Kate Bush Fan Club EP
...

c = format.

Numbering scheme changes from title to title. Within one title, all items on the same kind of material (e.g. all 7"'s, or all LPs, or all CDs etc.) have the same number at this position.

d = country.

Numbering scheme changes. Cover takes precedence if different from media. If cover isn't known, media is used. If both are unknown, the item is listed under a separate country entry. If just the general origin is known, it's listed under that country ('UK' instead of 'UK/UK' for example).

e = sequence number.

Lists various items with no other apparent difference in the above numbers.
a.b.c.d.1 = 1st entry,
a.b.c.d.2 = 2nd entry...
